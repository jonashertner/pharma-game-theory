Roche — the durability fight

20 May 2026 · confidential to the board · one page

The pricing fight is settled. The durability fight has begun. Three Roche-specific exposures — reference-pricing contagion, the trontinemab launch architecture, and the strategic purpose of the $50B US capex — carry two to five times the NPV of every remaining MFN/IRA debate combined, and are decided in 2026.

Reference-pricing contagion
CHF 18–55B
Trontinemab launch architecture
CHF 15–35B
Strategic purpose of $50B US capex
CHF 4–12B
5-year NPV swing (2027–2031). Bars proportional to upper-bound CHF. → defended in bottom-line

Where we stand

The 19 December 2025 MFN signing landed a stronger Roche outcome than peers: 0% Section 232 through January 2029, only Xofluza ceded, the five US growth drivers (Ocrevus, Hemlibra, Vabysmo, Xolair, Phesgo — CHF 21.4B FY25) kept out of MFN scope, only Xolair on IRA Round 3. The $50B US capex is underway. Pharma Division FY25: CHF 47.7B (+9% CER); US 53%; top-5 biologics concentrate 60% of US Pharma revenue.

What we will do

  1. Endorse the firewall annex with 60-day pre-conditions. Protect CHF 18–55B of 2027–2031 ex-US revenue against reference-pricing contagion. Decision: this board.
  2. Add trontinemab launch architecture to the Q3 2026 board agenda. NPV at stake: CHF 15–35B. The decision is launch sequence and pricing posture, not science.
  3. Re-segment the $50B US capex into tariff-defense vs franchise-growth tranches; reserve 20% small-molecule-convertible. NPV at stake: CHF 4–12B, with positive skew if Section 232 is renewed past 2029.

What we are watching

  • Reference-pricing spillover or EU forced disclosure — tail CHF 35–55B. Watch: AMNOG decisions through Q4 2027; EU pharmaceutical strategy implementation milestone Q3 2026.
  • Trontinemab Phase 3 platform safety — probability 10–20%; CHF 25–40B platform write-down. Watch: TRONTIER 1/2 interim safety reviews; comparable brain-shuttle readouts.
  • 2029 Section 232 sunset without renewal — probability 30–40%; CHF 4–10B stranded capex. Watch: 2026 midterms; 2028 primary structure; USTR personnel.

When we revisit

  • 30 June 2026 — firewall-annex pre-conditions resolved (USTR sounding, peer-firm peer-test, GC MRA-transfer memo).
  • 30 September 2026 — annex text status; trontinemab launch architecture decision frame.
  • Q4 2026 — $50B capex re-segmentation report.
  • 20 January 2029 — Section 232 sunset decision; if unrenewed, the strategic posture inverts and this memo is re-written.

If any two of the named tests resolve against the framework, the strategic posture should be revisited. If all three, the framework is wrong.

The thesis in one paragraph

The December 2025 MFN deal is signed. The negotiation has shifted from price-setting to durability engineering. Roche's single most important strategic asymmetry against every actor at the table is its multi-generational time horizon — every other principal operates on political-cycle or earnings-cycle timeframes. The 2026 task is to convert the existing framework into multi-decade pharma-pricing infrastructure while the political coalitions that produced it are still in place. The highest-value underused lever is international reference-pricing protection through a Swiss-annex amendment modelled on Germany's January 2025 Medical Research Act — Roche's highest-weight unused lever in the model. The biggest tail risk is reference-basket contagion into EU/UK/CH; treat as tail-risk insurance, not as expected-value optimisation.

Calibration

The model is calibrated against the actual December 2025 signed state. Six of twelve actors clear BATNA with positive surplus — Roche, Swiss Federal Council, Swiss cantons-Basel, Swiss public payers, Novartis, investors. Six sit exactly at BATNA — including, notably, both US actors (US Executive and US Congress), plus PBMs, patient advocacy, EU reference-pricing, biosimilars-competitors. The US-at-BATNA result is consistent with the “Roche won the MFN round” reading: the US Administration was essentially indifferent between the signed deal and a no-deal counterfactual. The deal is feasible (in the core); it is also fragile, because six actors have no surplus to defend. Verifiable test: python -m pytest model/tests/.

Where this could be wrong — three named contingencies

  1. The US Administration may not accept confidentiality language at all. The MFN program's narrative is "American patients should pay no more than other countries"; confidentiality language about ex-US prices could be characterised as protecting manufacturer-private information. Mitigation: 60-day USTR back-channel sounding before commitment.
  2. Treaty form may not survive the 2029 administration. The November 2025 framework is a Joint Statement, not a Senate-ratified treaty. Mitigation: structure the annex with treaty-adjacent legal form via TPA delegation or congressional-executive agreement.
  3. The Swiss Federal Council may not carry the cantons. The three-actor Swiss disaggregation is structurally real; Schinecker's October 2025 Swiss-price ask was a near-miss. Mitigation: explicit zero-anchor on Swiss domestic pricing in any annex.

What this is

The model layer outputs ordinal payoff weights. The bottom-line analysis compresses those into three CHF NPV asymmetries (reference-pricing contagion CHF 25–60B, trontinemab launch architecture CHF 20–30B, stranded US capex CHF 4–10B). It maps each of the 13 model issues onto Roche's top-12 marketed drugs and the trontinemab pipeline, computes 2027–2031 revenue paths under each decision-tree branch, and rolls up to portfolio impact.

The concentration story. The top-5 single-source biologics — Ocrevus, Hemlibra, Vabysmo, Xolair, Tecentriq — account for CHF 15.2B of US Pharma revenue, or 60% of the US Pharma total. The five exposures — how the 13 model issues map to drug economics

The model's 13 negotiation issues collapse into five distinct CHF-impact mechanisms when translated to product economics.

Exposure A — IRA Maximum Fair Price

Mapped from: Issue 2 (IRA MFP), partly Issue 10 (patent / pill-penalty), partly Issue 11 (IP).

Mechanism. Selected drug pays MFP as net price to Medicare beneficiaries; effective 7-year (small molecule) or 11-year (biologic) post-launch eligibility window; CMS-disclosed price cuts in Round 1 ranged from 38–79% off WAC.

Roche current exposure. Xolair only (Round 3, MFP effective 1 January 2028). Estimated MFP cut 50–65% off WAC based on Round 1/2 precedent. Annual revenue impact at Xolair scale: CHF 1.0–1.5B/year US revenue lost from 1 Jan 2028, partially offset by volume retention and partially compounded by Sept 2026 biosimilar entry.

Forward exposure. Future rounds: Vabysmo is the most likely next-round Part B selection (high Medicare spend, single-source, anti-VEGF class). Hemlibra is plausible Round 5+ selection if specialty Part D thresholds tighten. Ocrevus is plausible Round 6+ if biosimilar timing slips.

Exposure B — Section 232 tariff

Mapped from: Issue 3 (Section 232), Issue 4 (manufacturing footprint).

Mechanism. Tariff on Swiss-origin biologics imported into the US. Current rate under MFN-deal track: 0% through 20 January 2029. Statutory rate without deal: 100%. With US–Switzerland framework but no MFN deal: 15% ceiling.

Roche current exposure. All five top biologics (Ocrevus, Hemlibra, Vabysmo, Tecentriq, Xolair) plus Phesgo and Perjeta are largely Swiss-origin (Basel, Kaiseraugst, Penzberg). US revenue at risk = CHF 18B (sum of US lines for these seven). At 15% landed-cost surcharge (the no-deal fallback), the gross-margin hit would be CHF 2.7B/year. At 100% (full Section 232), economically unviable on these volumes — product would shift channels or pricing would be re-engineered.

Forward exposure. The 20 Jan 2029 sunset is a cliff. Renewal under a successor administration vs sunset is binary. The $50B US capex plan is designed to shift the manufacturing centre of gravity by 2029–2031, but the build is in tranches and is not complete by sunset.

Exposure C — MFN coverage and TrumpRx DTC

Mapped from: Issue 1 (MFN coverage), Issue 6 (TrumpRx).

Mechanism. MFN-implied net price applies to Medicaid (current scope) or expands to Medicare/commercial (forward scope creep). TrumpRx DTC channel offers manufacturer-discount platform.

Roche current exposure. None of the top-7 biologics is in MFN scope as of December 2025. Only Xofluza was ceded for Medicaid MFN access. The exposure here is forward, not current.

Forward exposure. If MFN scope expands to commercial markets (2028+ policy possibility), Vabysmo / Ocrevus / Hemlibra could be pulled in. At MFN-implied prices (referencing G7 average), the net-price haircut is 30–50% vs current WAC. The 2028 election outcome dominates this exposure.

Exposure D — International reference-pricing contagion

Mapped from: Issue 7 (international reference-pricing protection), Issue 12 (diplomatic carve-outs).

Mechanism. If US net prices (whether IRA-set, MFN-implied, or TrumpRx-disclosed) become legible to EU AMNOG, UK NICE, Swiss BAG, JP NHI, or other reference-pricing jurisdictions, the confidential-rebate architecture that supports current ex-US net prices erodes. The mechanism operates either as gradual leakage (each AMNOG cycle resets downward) or as forced disclosure (EU pharmaceutical strategy mandates transparency).

Roche current exposure. The largest single exposure. High-WAC biologics ex-US = CHF 7.3B/year (sum of global − US for the top six biologics). Spillover impact ranges:

  • Gradual spillover (low): 15–25% net-price erosion on confidential-rebate biologics ≈ CHF 1.1–1.8B/year EBIT impact.
  • Aggressive spillover (central): 30–40% net-price erosion ≈ CHF 2.2–2.9B/year.
  • EU forced disclosure (tail): full confidential-net exposure on broader ex-US specialty portfolio ≈ CHF 4–7B/year.

Plus second-order US drag: if reference-pricing contagion pulls down ex-US prices, US commercial channels following Medicare/Medicaid benchmarks see additional 0.5×–1.0× of the ex-US impact. Total annual EBIT swing thus CHF 2–11B/year depending on spillover scenario.

Exposure E — Biosimilar / loss-of-exclusivity

Mapped from: Issue 10 (patent / pill-penalty), Issue 11 (IP), market mechanics.

Mechanism. Patent expiry and biosimilar entry erode net price and volume. Acute erosion in first 3 years post-LOE; baseline reset thereafter.

Roche current exposure. Drugs already eroded: Avastin, Herceptin, MabThera/Rituxan (down 17–22% in 2025), Actemra (multiple biosimilars), Lucentis (Susvimo plus competitor biosims). Drugs facing entry in window: Xolair (Sept 2026 biosim launch). Drugs with long runway: Vabysmo (post-2032), Hemlibra (post-2030), Ocrevus (late 2030s in EU, biosim signal building in US).

Forward exposure. The Ocrevus biosim signal in 2030–2032 is the largest single LOE event in the portfolio — CHF 5B+ revenue at risk over 3 years post-entry depending on biosimilar uptake curves and Ocrevus SC formulation defensibility.

Per-drug deep dives — the five highest-leverage products

Ocrevus — CHF 7.0B, 70% US, the cornerstone

Ocrevus is 20% of Roche US Pharma revenue all by itself. The 2027–2031 base case assumes +6% CER growth (conservative vs the FY25 +9%) to a 2031 run-rate of CHF 9.4B, of which ~CHF 6.5B US.

Scenario impacts (5-year cumulative revenue, CHF):

  • Branch A (firewall executes): base case CHF 41B cumulative 2027–2031. Minor refp contagion, no MFN scope creep, Section 232 stays at 0%. Ocrevus SC franchise extends value.
  • Branch B (conditional): base − CHF 0.5–1B cumulative.
  • Branch C (defer to Q3 2026): base − CHF 1–2B cumulative (window of vulnerability extended).
  • Branch D (reject firewall): base − CHF 4–8B cumulative if severe spillover; up to −CHF 12B if EU forced disclosure occurs.

Why Ocrevus carries the most contagion risk in absolute terms. The combination of (a) highest US revenue line in the portfolio, (b) high confidential ex-US net prices (CHF 2.1B ex-US revenue at large per-patient WAC), and (c) Genentech-launched single-net-price model means a single AMNOG reset cascades quickly. The 2030–2032 biosimilar signal compounds the urgency — refp contagion in 2027–2029 reduces NPV that would otherwise be defended by SC formulation extension and lifecycle management.

Hemlibra — CHF 4.75B, 56% US, the specialty bellwether

Hemlibra's ~$500–700K/patient annual WAC sits at the extreme end of the confidential-rebate architecture. Ex-US net prices are a fraction of WAC. Forced disclosure or aggressive spillover hits Hemlibra harder in percentage terms than any other product.

Scenario impacts (5-year cumulative revenue, CHF):

  • Branch A: base case CHF 28B cumulative 2027–2031 (+10% CER assumed).
  • Branch D: −CHF 3–6B cumulative in severe-spillover; −CHF 8–12B in forced-disclosure tail.

Forward IRA risk. Hemlibra is a plausible Round 5+ specialty Part D candidate if CMS extends selection thresholds to small-population specialty drugs. The Round 3 selection of Xolair (a smaller population than typical Round 1/2 selections) signals CMS appetite for specialty.

Vabysmo — CHF 4.1B, 70% US, the IRA Part B target

The fastest-growing major (+12% CER). Most likely next-round IRA Part B candidate among Roche products: high Medicare Part B spend, single-source mechanism, growing patient population.

Scenario impacts (5-year cumulative revenue, CHF):

  • Branch A: base case CHF 27B cumulative (+12% CER held for 3 years, slowing to +6%).
  • Branch D + Vabysmo IRA Round 5 selection: −CHF 6–9B cumulative.

Susvimo franchise defense. The Susvimo refillable port extends franchise NPV but does not block IRA selection on the underlying anti-VEGF mechanism. The defensive move is launch differentiation under a new code rather than reliance on patent extension.

Xolair — CHF 3.08B US, the locked-in IRA hit

Xolair's IRA Round 3 selection is settled. MFP effective 1 January 2028. Biosimilar Omlyclo expected September 2026 — biosim erosion begins before IRA MFP takes effect.

Scenario impacts (5-year cumulative revenue, CHF):

  • All branches: CHF 8–11B cumulative 2027–2031 (down from a CHF 15B+ trajectory absent IRA + biosim).
  • Branch differentials are small for Xolair — the hit is locked in; the firewall annex does not change this.

Calibration value. Xolair is the test case for forward IRA cuts. If Xolair's effective net-price cut comes in at the low end of the Round 1/2 range (~40–50%), forward-IRA scenarios for Vabysmo/Hemlibra should be modelled toward the lower end; if at the high end (~70–80%), forward scenarios should be modelled toward the upper end.

The Perjeta + Phesgo franchise — CHF 5.4B combined, stable

Perjeta is declining (−13% CER) as Phesgo absorbs patients (+48% CER). Combined franchise stable at CHF 5.4B. The SC combo product has materially better COGS (subcutaneous vs IV infusion logistics) and similar net price — gross margin on the franchise is improving even though gross revenue is flat.

Scenario impacts. Lower than other top-5 because (a) combined franchise less exposed to refp contagion than the pure biologics, (b) US share is only 29% for Phesgo, lower asymmetry. Branch A vs D differential ~CHF 2–4B cumulative.

And the US capex strategic-purpose asymmetry, also orthogonal:

  • Section 232 renewed past 2029 (2028 populist successor scenario): +CHF 6–12B capex revaluation as tariff insurance.
  • Section 232 sunsets without renewal: −CHF 4–10B carrying-cost-only asset.

Calibration vs the bottom-line claims

The bottom-line analysis claimed three asymmetry ranges. The financial-translation math supports them with the following refinements:

  1. Reference-pricing contagion: claimed CHF 25–60B NPV swing → refined to CHF 18–55B over 2027–2031, with the band splitting as: - Mild spillover: CHF 18–28B (the central case under Branch D). - Severe spillover or forced disclosure: CHF 35–55B. - The CHF 60B upper bound is only reachable in a forced-disclosure scenario that combines black swan #3 (EU transparency mandate) with Branch D. Probability-weighted, the expected contagion exposure is closer to CHF 22–30B.

  2. Trontinemab launch architecture: claimed CHF 20–30B NPV swing → confirmed at CHF 15–35B, with the band splitting as: - Launch pre-IRA-eligible vs IRA-constrained: CHF 15–20B. - Launch successful vs Phase 3 platform failure: CHF 25–40B. - The two scenarios are not additive; treat as alternative paths.

  3. Strategic purpose of the $50B US capex: claimed CHF 4–10B NPV swing → confirmed at CHF 4–12B, with upside skew if 2028 election produces extended tariff regime.

Net. The bottom-line ranges hold up against bottom-up product math, with the contagion claim trimmed slightly at the high end (the CHF 60B was only reachable in tail-conditional scenarios). The trontinemab and capex ranges are confirmed.

What this changes for the 2026 board agenda

Three implications fall directly out of the financial-translation work:

  1. The top-5 single-source biologics are 60% of US Pharma revenue. This concentration is the lever the firewall annex protects. The Item 1 unpacked action plan should reference this concentration explicitly as the asset under protection — not the contagion risk abstractly.

  2. Vabysmo is the most likely next-round IRA Part B selection. A 2026 work-stream should pre-stage Vabysmo's forward-IRA defence: differentiation through Susvimo, indication sequencing, payer-channel optionality, and pre-negotiation with CMS through industry channels.

  3. The Perjeta → Phesgo substitution is a quiet win — it should not be quietly absorbed. The franchise economics improved meaningfully (lower COGS, similar net price) without any 2026 decision. Communicate this to investors as a portfolio-management capability example, supporting the credibility of forward franchise-defence claims (Ocrevus SC, Tecentriq Hybreza, Vabysmo Susvimo).
