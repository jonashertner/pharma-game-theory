US drug-pricing programmes

MFN — Most-Favored-Nation drug pricing. A Trump-administration policy (Executive Order 14297, signed 12 May 2025) requiring US prices for designated drugs to match the lowest price among OECD countries with GDP per capita ≥ 60% of US GDP. As of 19 May 2026, 17 manufacturers have signed voluntary MFN agreements; the alternative is mandatory MFN via subsequent rule-making plus Section 232 tariff exposure.

IRA — Inflation Reduction Act. US law signed August 2022. The drug-pricing provisions establish Medicare's first-ever direct price negotiation authority for high-spend drugs. Selected drugs receive a "Maximum Fair Price" (MFP). Rounds: Round 1 (10 drugs, prices effective 1 January 2026), Round 2 (15 drugs, prices effective 1 January 2027), Round 3 (15 drugs including Xolair, prices effective 1 January 2028).

MFP — Maximum Fair Price. The negotiated Medicare price published by CMS under the IRA negotiation process. Typically ~50–60% below pre-negotiation list price for first-round drugs.

Section 232. Section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act of 1962 authorises the President to impose tariffs on imports that threaten national security. The 2 April 2026 proclamation extended Section 232 to pharmaceutical imports with a four-tier rate structure (100% / 20% / 15% / 0%). The 0% tier requires a signed MFN deal plus US-capacity build; this tier sunsets 20 January 2029.

TrumpRx. A direct-to-consumer drug pricing platform launched 5 February 2026 (trumprx.gov). Manufacturers sign onto the platform offering specified drugs at MFN-aligned prices, paid directly by consumers; intended to bypass the PBM rebate-arbitrage layer. Initial cohort: 43 drugs.

Pill penalty. Common name for the IRA's asymmetric small-molecule vs biologic timing: small-molecule drugs become eligible for IRA negotiation 9 years after FDA approval; biologics 13 years. The 4-year gap creates a competitive disadvantage for small-molecule innovation. The EPIC Act (S. 832) seeks to soften this.

Bayh-Dole march-in. Provisions of the Bayh-Dole Act (1980) that allow the federal government to compel licensing of drugs whose development received federal funding. Periodically invoked rhetorically by administrations seeking to pressure drug-price negotiations; not used in practice to date.

PBM — Pharmacy Benefit Manager. Intermediary that manages prescription-drug benefits for insurers, employers, and government programmes. The Big Three (Caremark/CVS, Express Scripts/Cigna, OptumRx/UnitedHealth) cover ~80% of US prescription claims. Profit historically from rebate arbitrage; the 3 February 2026 Consolidated Appropriations Act delinks Medicare Part D PBM compensation from rebates, effective 2028-29.

340B. US programme requiring drug manufacturers to provide discounts to safety-net hospitals. Important for understanding biosimilar competitive economics.

BPCIA — Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act. US law establishing the regulatory pathway for biosimilar approval. Includes a 12-year exclusivity for originator biologics and a 180-day interchangeable exclusivity for the first biosimilar.

Swiss drug-pricing system

BAG — Bundesamt für Gesundheit / Federal Office of Public Health. Swiss federal authority responsible for the Spezialitätenliste (SL) — the list of reimbursed medicines under LAMal/KVG. Sets prices via triennial international + therapeutic comparison.

LAMal / KVG. Swiss mandatory health insurance law (Loi sur l'Assurance Maladie / Krankenversicherungsgesetz). Article 52a establishes the price-setting authority delegated to BAG.

SL — Spezialitätenliste / Specialty List. The Swiss list of reimbursed medicines under LAMal. BAG reviews prices triennially using a nine-country reference basket (DE, FR, AT, BE, NL, DK, FI, SE, UK).

Santésuisse / curafutura. Swiss health-insurer industry associations. Santésuisse represents the majority of insurers; curafutura includes Helsana, CSS, Sanitas, KPT.

Bundesrat. The Swiss Federal Council — a seven-member collegial executive cabinet. As of 2026, President is Guy Parmelin (also Economics Minister leading SECO).

SECO. State Secretariat for Economic Affairs. The Swiss federal department responsible for trade negotiations (including the US framework).

Kantonal / Cantonal. Refers to Switzerland's 26 cantonal governments. Basel-Stadt and Basel-Landschaft are the host cantons for Roche; their interests are documented separately from the federal Bundesrat because Swiss federalism gives cantons substantial fiscal and policy autonomy.

EU drug-pricing & regulatory

EMA — European Medicines Agency. The EU's central medicines regulator. Reviews drug applications for centralised marketing authorisation valid across the EU.

G-BA — Gemeinsamer Bundesausschuss / Federal Joint Committee. German federal body that assesses the added clinical benefit of new drugs (using IQWiG analysis) and sets the AMNOG benefit-assessment outcome.

IQWiG — Institut für Qualität und Wirtschaftlichkeit im Gesundheitswesen. German Institute for Quality and Efficiency in Health Care. Produces the benefit-assessment analysis that informs G-BA decisions.

AMNOG. German law (Arzneimittelmarktneuordnungsgesetz, 2011) establishing the early benefit-assessment process for new drugs. The negotiated Erstattungsbetrag is the reimbursement price agreed between the manufacturer and the SHI umbrella organisation.

MRA / MFG — German Medical Research Act / Medizinforschungsgesetz. German legislation in force from 1 January 2025 introducing a confidential Erstattungsbetrag option (with a 9% additional discount and R&D-in-Germany requirement, sunsetting 30 June 2028). The structural template the recommendation's "firewall annex" proposes to mirror.

Erstattungsbetrag. The negotiated reimbursement price for a drug under German AMNOG. Historically public; the 2025 MRA allows confidential alternative.

HAS — Haute Autorité de Santé. French national health authority. The Transparency Commission within HAS assesses drugs (SMR / ASMR ratings) before pricing negotiation with CEPS.

CEPS — Comité Économique des Produits de Santé. French inter-ministerial price-negotiation committee. Negotiates drug prices with manufacturers based on HAS assessments.

SMR / ASMR. French drug benefit ratings: SMR (Service Médical Rendu) assesses absolute clinical benefit; ASMR (Amélioration du Service Médical Rendu) assesses added benefit vs comparators.

NICE — National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. UK NHS health-technology-assessment body. Issues Technology Appraisals (TAs) that determine NHS reimbursement.

AIFA — Agenzia Italiana del Farmaco. Italian medicines agency. Manages Managed Entry Agreements (MEAs) and registry-based outcome-tracking for new drugs.

JCA — Joint Clinical Assessment. EU-level clinical assessment process under HTA Regulation 2021/2282. Live since 12 January 2025 for oncology and Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products (ATMPs). Will expand to orphan drugs in 2028 and all new medicines in 2030.

CMA — Critical Medicines Act. EU regulation, provisional trilogue agreement 12 May 2026. Establishes mechanisms for ensuring supply resilience of critical medicines in the EU, including manufacturing-incentive provisions.

EFPIA — European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations. Brussels-based industry trade association for innovator pharmaceutical companies in Europe.

China drug-pricing

NHSA — National Healthcare Security Administration. Chinese central agency (established 2018) responsible for national health insurance reimbursement and drug-price negotiation.

NRDL — National Reimbursement Drug List. China's list of drugs reimbursed under national health insurance. Annual negotiation rounds determine listings and prices. The 2025 round concluded with 88% inclusion success rate and an average 14% renewal cut on previously-listed drugs.

VBP — Volume-Based Procurement. China's centralised tender mechanism for generic and biosimilar drugs. Began with the "4+7" pilot in 2018; rolled out nationally in subsequent rounds. The 11th national round was held October 2025.

NMPA — National Medical Products Administration. Chinese drug-and-device regulatory authority (formerly CFDA).

Game-theoretic concepts

BATNA — Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. In bargaining theory, the actor's expected outcome if no deal is reached. Sets the floor below which the actor will walk.

Nash bargaining. A cooperative game-theory solution concept that selects the deal maximising the product (or weighted sum of logarithms) of all parties' surplus above BATNA. Used in the model as the primary equilibrium concept.

Kalai-Smorodinsky. An alternative cooperative game-theory concept that maximises the minimum proportional surplus across parties. Used in the model as a fairness-emphasising alternative to Nash.

Shapley value. A solution concept that attributes value to each actor based on their marginal contribution across all possible coalitions. Used in the model to surface which actors are structurally central vs politically loud.

Core. A cooperative game-theory stability concept: a deal is "in the core" if no sub-coalition of actors can profitably defect. The model checks proposed deals against the core to identify instability.

Red line. In this analysis: a value of an issue beyond which an actor will not accept any deal regardless of other terms. Distinct from BATNA: BATNA is the no-deal outcome; red lines describe deal-acceptability constraints.

MFC — Most-Favored-Customer clauses. Contractual provisions where a seller guarantees a buyer the lowest price offered to any comparable buyer. The private-contract analog to MFN. Considered in the novelty engine as a potential firewall mechanism.

CAC — Collective Action Clauses. Provisions in sovereign-debt contracts that allow a supermajority of creditors to bind dissenting holdouts. Considered in the novelty engine as an analog mechanism for binding MFN signatories to comparable treatment.

Other terms-of-art

EPC — Engineering, Procurement, Construction. Contract structure common in major construction projects where payments are gated by independently certified milestones. Used in the recommendation as the model for structuring Roche's $50B US capex commitment with milestone-gated tariff relief.

Erstattungsbetrag. See Germany. (German AMNOG-negotiated reimbursement price.)

CRS — Congressional Research Service. Non-partisan US legislative research body that produces reports for Congress. CRS LSB11319 covers the MFN Executive Order's legal issues; R47872 covers IRA industry responses.

CBO — Congressional Budget Office. Non-partisan US legislative budget-and-policy analysis body. Produces baseline scoring of legislative drug-pricing provisions.

8-K / 10-K / 20-F. US SEC filings: 8-K is a current report disclosing material events; 10-K is an annual report (US filers); 20-F is the annual report for foreign private issuers (e.g., Novartis files 20-F).

6-K. SEC filing form used by foreign private issuers (e.g., Roche, Novartis) to disclose material events between annual reports.

ADR — American Depositary Receipt. US-traded certificate representing shares in a foreign company (e.g., Roche's RHHBY).

Genussschein / ROP. Roche's non-voting participation certificate. The original Genussschein was converted to a new "ROP Participation Certificate" in March 2026 (new ISIN CH1499059983).
