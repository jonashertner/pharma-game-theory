The thesis in one paragraph

The December 2025 MFN deal is signed. The negotiation has shifted from price-setting to durability engineering. Roche's single most important strategic asymmetry against every actor at the table is its multi-generational time horizon — every other principal operates on political-cycle or earnings-cycle timeframes. The 2026 task is to convert the existing framework into multi-decade pharma-pricing infrastructure while the political coalitions that produced it are still in place. The highest-value underused lever is international reference-pricing protection through a Swiss-annex amendment modelled on Germany's January 2025 Medical Research Act — Roche's highest-weight unused lever in the model. The biggest tail risk is reference-basket contagion into EU/UK/CH; treat as tail-risk insurance, not as expected-value optimisation.

Calibration

The model is calibrated against the actual December 2025 signed state. Six of twelve actors clear BATNA with positive surplus — Roche, Swiss Federal Council, Swiss cantons-Basel, Swiss public payers, Novartis, investors. Six sit exactly at BATNA — including, notably, both US actors (US Executive and US Congress), plus PBMs, patient advocacy, EU reference-pricing, biosimilars-competitors. The US-at-BATNA result is consistent with the “Roche won the MFN round” reading: the US Administration was essentially indifferent between the signed deal and a no-deal counterfactual. The deal is feasible (in the core); it is also fragile, because six actors have no surplus to defend. Verifiable test: python -m pytest model/tests/.

Where this could be wrong — three named contingencies

  1. The US Administration may not accept confidentiality language at all. The MFN program's narrative is "American patients should pay no more than other countries"; confidentiality language about ex-US prices could be characterised as protecting manufacturer-private information. Mitigation: 60-day USTR back-channel sounding before commitment.
  2. Treaty form may not survive the 2029 administration. The November 2025 framework is a Joint Statement, not a Senate-ratified treaty. Mitigation: structure the annex with treaty-adjacent legal form via TPA delegation or congressional-executive agreement.
  3. The Swiss Federal Council may not carry the cantons. The three-actor Swiss disaggregation is structurally real; Schinecker's October 2025 Swiss-price ask was a near-miss. Mitigation: explicit zero-anchor on Swiss domestic pricing in any annex.

Decision tree — what each board response triggers

Four possible board responses to Items 1–4. What follows for each.

Branch A — Endorse all four items
Items 1, 2 endorsed (conditional on 60-day pre-conditions). Item 3 decided (commit or defer). Item 4 noted.

What happens next.

  • 60-day pre-condition window opens (May 20 – Jun 30). USTR back-channel, peer-firm sounding, GC MRA-transfer memo all in flight.
  • If pre-conditions pass: full annex negotiation Q3 2026 with SECO leading. Pharma Hansa, Federalist Papers, Mandate-of-Heaven ceremony, wabi-sabi excess all proceed in parallel.
  • CHF 8–12M government-affairs budget released. CEO Schinecker + General Counsel + Head of Government Affairs engaged personally.
  • +$5B capex flexibility window opens for 2025–2027 deployment.
  • Item 3 status drives patient-access communications strategy through 2026 H2.
  • Next board re-engagement: 30 Sep 2026 (annex text status) or sooner if pre-conditions fail.
Branch B — Conditional endorsement
Items 1, 2 conditionally endorsed pending pre-condition tests. Items 3, 4 same as Branch A.

What happens next.

  • 60-day pre-condition window opens but without full commitment. Pre-conditions tested as in Branch A.
  • No budget release until pre-conditions pass and board re-engages.
  • Parallel-track work (Hansa, Federalist Papers, AI-infrastructure, communications discipline) proceeds at reduced resourcing.
  • Mandate-of-Heaven ceremony and wabi-sabi excess proceed (low-cost, no incremental commitment).
  • Next board re-engagement: 30 Jun 2026 mandatory, with pre-condition results in hand.
  • Risk: Q3 2026 annex closure window slips to Q1 2027 due to delayed commitment.
Branch C — Defer Items 1+2 to Q3 2026
Items 1, 2 deferred for further analysis. Items 3, 4 same as Branch A.

What happens next.

  • Annex window closes for 2026; renegotiation framing accepted for Q1 2027.
  • Pre-conditions still tested in parallel (low cost; informs Q3 2026 re-engagement).
  • Mandate-of-Heaven ceremony, wabi-sabi excess, Pharma Hansa proceed (do not require firewall-annex commitment).
  • Federalist Papers campaign commissioned (long lead time; better to start regardless).
  • AI-augmented negotiation infrastructure build proceeds (window closes regardless of Items 1+2).
  • Next board re-engagement: Q3 2026 with refined Item 1 (Item 1 unpacked) and updated pre-condition results.
Branch D — Reject firewall-annex approach
Item 1 rejected. Items 2, 3, 4 considered separately.

What happens next.

  • Fallback plan triggers within 30 days. Choice among four fallbacks (see Item 1 unpacked):
    • Fallback A — Congressional vehicle. Firewall framework moves to EPIC Act or successor 2027 legislation.
    • Fallback B — EU-side firewall. Enhanced MRA pursuit in DE/FR/UK; ex-EU price protection only.
    • Fallback C — Commercial-contract confidentiality. Operational-level tightening across all jurisdictions.
    • Fallback D — Wait. Defer to 2027 successor administration if 2026 is unfavourable.
  • Item 2 (+$5B capex flexibility) re-considered on its own merits — potentially endorsed even without Item 1, as capex flexibility has value regardless.
  • Cross-domain moves (Hansa, Federalist Papers, Mycorrhizal, etc.) proceed independently of firewall decision.
  • Next board re-engagement: 30 Aug 2026 with fallback-plan execution status.
