Overview

Bottom line

To: Roche Executive Committee · From: Strategic-analysis working group · Date: 20 May 2026 · Read time: 4 minutes

The December 2025 MFN deal is settled cost. Whether it pays off is decided by three 2026 choices that have nothing to do with MFN — the firewall annex, the trontinemab launch architecture, and the strategic purpose of the $50B US capex. Together these carry two to five times the NPV of every remaining MFN/IRA debate combined.

What was actually won in December 2025

The MFN-signing wave produced a specific Roche outcome that should be read precisely, not as "we caved" or "we won":

  • 0% Section 232 tariff through 20 January 2029 (versus 100% statutory).
  • Xofluza ceded for Medicaid MFN access — a low-importance asset in exchange for a high-importance political-cover concession.
  • Vabysmo, Ocrevus, Hemlibra, Tecentriq, Phesgo kept out of MFN coverage — the entire 2025 US growth-driver portfolio remains at WAC pricing.
  • Only Xolair on IRA Round 3 — co-marketed with Novartis, so the IRA dollar impact is shared, not borne alone.
  • No public naming/shaming — Roche signed in the first wave (19 December 2025), not under pressure later like the holdouts.
  • 3 years bought at 0% tariff to build US capacity at the $50B scale already announced.

This is a strong outcome relative to the universe of plausible December 2025 outcomes. The signing was not a defensive concession; it was the consolidation of a position negotiated through six months of Swiss-state diplomatic intervention (the 14 November 2025 framework) and Roche's own $50B capex pre-commitment (announced 22 April 2025).

Why the next four years are larger, not smaller

The instinct after a major settlement is to declare victory and demobilise. That would be a category error here. The 2025 deal compresses the pricing fight into the rules of the road for 2026–2030. Three of those rules are still being written, and Roche's exposure to each is asymmetric to its peers.

Asymmetry 1 — Reference-pricing contagion (CHF 25–60B NPV swing)

The exposure. If US MFN-implied net prices leak into EU AMNOG, UK NICE, Swiss BAG, or JP NHI reference-pricing mechanisms, Roche bears the spillover at roughly two to three times the peer-firm rate because three exposures stack at Roche specifically: (a) the franchise is concentrated in single-source biologics priced at $5K+/month WAC where confidential ex-US net prices are the difference between viable and non-viable EU economics; (b) Switzerland is itself a reference-pricing jurisdiction with a 3-year BAG list review cycle, and the Swiss MFN spillover runs both directions; (c) the company is Swiss-headquartered, so EU/UK confidential-rebate disclosure regimes apply differently than they do to US-domiciled peers.

The lever. The Swiss-annex firewall amendment (Item 1 unpacked) is the highest-leverage 2026 move on this exposure. Estimated annual EBIT swing CHF 8–15B between a contagion-blocked and a contagion-leaked world; over 2027–2031 the NPV swing is CHF 25–60B.

Asymmetry 2 — Trontinemab launch architecture (CHF 20–30B NPV swing)

The exposure. Trontinemab Phase 3 readouts arrive in 2028+. The launch will land in a pricing world that did not exist when lecanemab and donanemab were designed. If trontinemab launches as an IRA-eligible Part D biologic into an MFN-extended pricing regime, peak revenue NPV is 40–60% of the un-constrained counterfactual. The full Alzheimer's franchise depends on this launch sequence; the brain-shuttle platform's value as a class extends well beyond trontinemab itself.

The lever. Decisions made in 2026–2027 about launch timing (full-speed-into-IRA versus deferral for political clarity), pricing architecture (one-time-payment versus annual-WAC), and indication sequencing (mild AD first versus full mild-moderate label) shape this NPV more than any pricing-deal renegotiation could. The board's influence is high; the topic is not on the board agenda yet.

Asymmetry 3 — The strategic purpose of the $50B US capex (CHF 4–10B NPV swing)

The exposure. The April 2025 $50B US capex pledge is currently justified internally as tariff defense and Section 232 cover. If the 2028 election produces an administration that lets Section 232 sunset (20 January 2029) and does not renew it, the capacity build becomes a carrying-cost-only asset rather than a growth platform. Conversely, if the political pressure that produced Section 232 hardens (the populist-successor scenario in black swans), the capex is worth 1.5–2× what it is today.

The lever. The board's 2026 decision is not whether to build — that ship has sailed — but how to frame and segment the build. Should at least 20% of new capacity be small-molecule-convertible to hedge biologics-displacement risk (black swan #4)? Should milestone gating tie capex tranches to specific tariff-architecture outcomes? These are 2026 choices.

What would prove this analysis wrong

A sharpened bottom line should name its own falsification conditions.

  1. If reference-pricing contagion does not materialise by end of 2027 (no AMNOG/NICE/BAG net-price leakage observed), the firewall annex urgency falls and Asymmetry 1 collapses by 60–80%. Test: quarterly AMNOG decisions through Q4 2027.

  2. If trontinemab Phase 3 reads out negative or with mechanism-related safety, Asymmetry 2 collapses entirely; pipeline NPV redistributes to non-brain-shuttle programs. Test: TRONTIER 1/2 interim safety readouts (timing per data/timeline.yaml).

  3. If a successor administration in 2029 dismantles Section 232 and unwinds MFN, the $50B US capex sits stranded and Asymmetry 3 flips negative. Test: January 2029 administration posture; Section 232 sunset disposition.

If any two of these tests resolve against the framework, the strategic posture should be revisited. If all three resolve against, the framework is wrong.

The implication for the board

The board's three highest-leverage 2026 decisions are not the four agenda items as currently framed. They are:

  1. Endorse the firewall annex (covered — Item 1 on briefing).
  2. Add trontinemab launch architecture to the Q3 2026 board agenda — not yet on it. The decision can be deferred into 2027, but the framing should be set this year.
  3. Re-segment the $50B US capex into tariff-defense versus franchise-growth tranches — same. The capital is committed; the strategic logic of each tranche is still movable.

Reference-pricing contagion is asymmetric downside. Trontinemab is asymmetric upside. The US capex is the structural bet on which administration sits in the White House in 2029. The 2026 board agenda should make all three visible, not just the first.
