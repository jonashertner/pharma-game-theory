## The thesis in one paragraph

The December 2025 MFN deal is signed. The negotiation has shifted from price-setting to *durability engineering*. Roche's single most important strategic asymmetry against every actor at the table is its multi-generational time horizon &mdash; every other principal operates on political-cycle or earnings-cycle timeframes. The 2026 task is to convert the existing framework into multi-decade pharma-pricing infrastructure while the political coalitions that produced it are still in place. The highest-value underused lever is international reference-pricing protection through a Swiss-annex amendment modelled on Germany's January 2025 Medical Research Act &mdash; Roche's highest-weight unused lever in the model. The biggest tail risk is reference-basket contagion into EU/UK/CH; treat as tail-risk insurance, not as expected-value optimisation.

## Calibration

The model is calibrated against the actual December 2025 signed state. Six of twelve actors clear BATNA with positive surplus &mdash; Roche, Swiss Federal Council, Swiss cantons-Basel, Swiss public payers, Novartis, investors. Six sit exactly at BATNA &mdash; **including, notably, both US actors (US Executive and US Congress)**, plus PBMs, patient advocacy, EU reference-pricing, biosimilars-competitors. The US-at-BATNA result is consistent with the &ldquo;Roche won the MFN round&rdquo; reading: the US Administration was essentially indifferent between the signed deal and a no-deal counterfactual. The deal is feasible (in the core); it is also fragile, because six actors have no surplus to defend. Verifiable test: `python -m pytest model/tests/`.

## Where this could be wrong &mdash; three named contingencies

1. *The US Administration may not accept confidentiality language at all.* The MFN program's narrative is "American patients should pay no more than other countries"; confidentiality language about ex-US prices could be characterised as protecting manufacturer-private information. **Mitigation:** 60-day USTR back-channel sounding before commitment.
2. *Treaty form may not survive the 2029 administration.* The November 2025 framework is a Joint Statement, not a Senate-ratified treaty. **Mitigation:** structure the annex with treaty-adjacent legal form via TPA delegation or congressional-executive agreement.
3. *The Swiss Federal Council may not carry the cantons.* The three-actor Swiss disaggregation is structurally real; Schinecker's October 2025 Swiss-price ask was a near-miss. **Mitigation:** explicit zero-anchor on Swiss domestic pricing in any annex.
