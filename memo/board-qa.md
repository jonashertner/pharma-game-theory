## Format

Fifteen questions a sophisticated board member would ask after reading the [one-pager](one-pager.html), [briefing](briefing.html), and [bottom line](bottom-line.html). Each answered in 2&ndash;4 sentences. Designed to be skimmed; designed to be re-read; designed to leave no question of substance unaddressed.

Questions are ordered by frame: framing &amp; timing &rarr; mechanism &amp; alternatives &rarr; risks &amp; uncertainties &rarr; governance &amp; communications.

---

### 1. Why now? Why not last year, why not next year?

December 2025 was a phase transition. Before the MFN signing, the negotiation was about price-setting; after, it is about durability. The 2026 window is unique because the political coalitions that produced the framework remain in place but will start dissolving into 2028 election positioning by Q1 2027. The 60-day pre-condition test on Item 1 is calibrated to this window: action this year is high-leverage; action in 2027 is lower-leverage; action in 2028 is too late.

### 2. Why the firewall annex specifically? Why not lobby AMNOG/NICE individually?

Bilateral lobbying scales linearly with the number of regulators and re-sets each cycle (AMNOG 3 years, NICE rolling). A treaty-form annex with confidentiality provisions scales as **institutional protection that survives political turnover** &mdash; one negotiation produces durable structure rather than thirty annual negotiations. The Hatch-Waxman 1984 analog: pharma traded specific concessions for durable institutional architecture that has shaped pricing for forty years. The firewall annex is structured to be this generation&apos;s analog.

### 3. What if the Swiss Federal Council won&apos;t carry the annex?

Pre-condition test #3 (SECO MRA-transfer memo) is the early-warning indicator. If SECO won&apos;t carry, the annex collapses to a US-CH bilateral framework adjustment without treaty form. Lower NPV (CHF 8&ndash;15B vs CHF 18&ndash;55B) but still net positive vs no protection. Decision rule: if SECO posture is negative by 30 June 2026, switch from Branch A to Branch B execution per the [decision-quality framework](decision-quality.html). The fallback is real; it is not a binary success/failure.

### 4. What if peers (Novartis, Pfizer, AstraZeneca) undermine the annex by signing their own?

A peer signing a separate annex does not undermine Roche&apos;s &mdash; pricing-protection annexes are non-rivalrous in legal structure. The actual peer risk is **a peer signing an annex that has weaker protection than Roche&apos;s, becoming the AMNOG benchmark**. Mitigation: industry-level coordination through EFPIA, PhRMA, and IFPMA on minimum-protection standards for any bilateral annex any peer pursues. Pre-condition test #2 surfaces this signal.

### 5. What&apos;s the political-cycle risk if a 2028 administration disowns the annex?

A successor administration cannot unilaterally void a Senate-ratified treaty or a TPA-delegated congressional-executive agreement. The annex&apos;s durability depends entirely on its legal form. If structured as a Joint Statement (like the November 2025 US&ndash;Switzerland framework), it is at-will and exposed to political reversal. If structured as a TPA-delegated congressional-executive agreement under the existing trade authority, it is durable to administration turnover. **The legal-form decision is the single most consequential drafting choice in Item 1** and is currently underspecified in working-group output; see [Item 1 unpacked](item-1.html).

### 6. Why CHF 18&ndash;55B and not a specific number?

The width reflects the unresolved scenario question &mdash; gradual contagion (CHF 18&ndash;28B over 2027&ndash;2031) versus EU-forced-disclosure tail (CHF 35&ndash;55B). Probability-weighted expected value is closer to CHF 22&ndash;30B per the [decision-quality framework](decision-quality.html). The range is honest about scenario uncertainty; a point estimate would be false precision. The board should make decisions on the central case; the tail is what the [black-swans analysis](black-swans.html) prepares against.

### 7. How is trontinemab launch architecture different from what every CEO already does?

Two structural differences. **First**, the decision is multi-year (launch timing &times; pricing posture &times; indication sequencing together), not annual revenue-target setting; this is the kind of decision board strategy committees structure, not management quarter-cycle activity. **Second**, the *optionality value of deferring* launch by 6&ndash;12 months for political clarity is on the table for the first time. Most pharma boards reflexively maximise launch speed; the trontinemab calculus may favour patience. The 2026 board should set the framing; the 2027 board makes the decision.

### 8. What&apos;s the second-best option if the firewall annex fails?

Branch B (conditional endorsement) per the [decision-quality framework](decision-quality.html) is the immediate fallback, with EV CHF 10B vs Branch A&apos;s EV CHF 8.7B. Beyond Branch B: industry-coalition lobbying for ex-US pricing-transparency *protection* at OECD/WHO level &mdash; the public-good move where Roche disproportionately benefits but cannot lead alone. Tertiary fallback: drug-by-drug AMNOG dossiers with formula-anchored confidentiality clauses, executed bilaterally with the German Federal Joint Committee and analogous bodies in 6&ndash;8 priority markets.

### 9. Why 20% small-molecule-convertible capacity, not 30% or 10%?

The capex is committed for biologics. Convertibility costs a ~5&ndash;8% premium on the affected tranches. 20% is sized to cover an analog-displacement scenario where 1&ndash;2 top biologics are displaced over 2027&ndash;2032, not a sector-wide displacement (which would require 50%+ convertibility, an unreasonable hedge cost). 30% would over-insure against a low-probability tail; 10% would not materially change the displacement outcome. The 20% figure is expected-value-optimised against the [black swan #5](black-swans.html) probability range; see [financial translation](financial-translation.html) assumption set.

### 10. What&apos;s the historical analog where this kind of move actually worked?

The 1984 Hatch-Waxman Drug Price Competition and Patent Term Restoration Act. Pharma accepted accelerated generic entry (the concession analog of MFN-Medicaid) in exchange for patent-term restoration and the Bolar exemption (the institutional-architecture analog of the firewall annex). The result: forty years of durable rules that survived multiple administrations and Congresses. The pattern is **trade specific revenue for durable institutional structure when the political coalition will let you**. Secondary analog: the 1993&ndash;94 Clinton Healthcare Reform &rarr; pharma response &rarr; BBA 1997 modulation. See planned forthcoming historical analog deep-dive (Tier 2 in the work backlog).

### 11. Why isn&apos;t there an M&amp;A recommendation?

The current analysis is focused on defending existing franchise NPV. M&amp;A becomes relevant in two specific scenarios. **First**, acquiring AI-native biotech to hedge [black swan #5](black-swans.html) (biologics displacement by AI-designed small molecules) &mdash; targeted CHF 1&ndash;3B acquisitions over 2026&ndash;2028 in companies with Phase-2-stage candidates against Roche&apos;s biologic targets. **Second**, acquiring CNS pipeline to diversify [black swan #4](black-swans.html) (trontinemab platform safety risk) &mdash; the universe is small (Denali, Ionis-CNS), but two named candidates exist. A separate M&amp;A working group is warranted and not yet stood up.

### 12. Should we cut the dividend to fund any of this?

No. The CHF 350&ndash;700M annualised cost of the robust-position portfolio (see [black-swans](black-swans.html) consolidated table) is &lt;2% of EBIT and well within free cash flow. The $50B capex program is the larger commitment (~CHF 9B/yr), also covered by FCF without dividend impact. **The dividend is itself a credible signal of long-horizon discipline.** Cutting it would damage the multi-generational time-horizon asymmetry that anchors the analysis, sending exactly the wrong signal to investors and the family pool.

### 13. What does the generational-shareholder pool think?

The 97.75% support for Schwan&apos;s re-election in March 2026 is the most current operational signal of pool alignment with the current strategic direction. The founding-family voting structure is the underlying premise of the multi-generational time-horizon asymmetry running through the entire analytical framework. Any major strategic move &mdash; the firewall annex commitment, the capex re-segmentation, the trontinemab launch decision &mdash; should be socialised through usual governance channels before board commitment. Pre-condition test for Item 1 should add: pool consultation by 15 July 2026.

### 14. Where could the analysis be wrong, in priority order?

Three named conditions per [bottom-line falsification clauses](bottom-line.html), in priority order:

1. **If reference-pricing contagion does not materialise by end of 2027** &mdash; quarterly AMNOG decisions through Q4 2027 are the test. If no leakage observed, Asymmetry 1 collapses 60&ndash;80%.
2. **If trontinemab Phase 3 reads negative or with mechanism-related safety** &mdash; TRONTIER 1/2 interim safety reviews are the test. Asymmetry 2 collapses entirely.
3. **If a 2029 administration dismantles Section 232 and unwinds MFN** &mdash; January 2029 administration posture is the test. Asymmetry 3 flips negative.

**Any two = framework revisit. All three = framework wrong.**

### 15. What should we tell investors at the next earnings call?

The Q3 2026 earnings call (October 2026) is the right moment for messaging, calibrated to follow the 30 September annex-text milestone. Three lines, in order:

1. **&ldquo;We secured the December 2025 deal at industry-leading terms.&rdquo;** (Validated by [peer comparison](peer-comparison.html); cite Xofluza-only cession and 0% Section 232 rate.)
2. **&ldquo;We are converting the framework into durable institutional architecture via bilateral protection mechanisms.&rdquo;** (Carefully worded; do not name &ldquo;firewall annex&rdquo; publicly until ratification path is clearer.)
3. **&ldquo;Our pipeline (trontinemab) and capacity build position us for the 2030+ decade.&rdquo;** (Sets the long-horizon narrative.)

**Avoid in public messaging:** the specific CHF impact ranges (analyst noise; provides anchor points for negative-surprise comparisons), the specific probability assignments from [decision quality](decision-quality.html) (sets up callbacks if probabilities update), and the [black swans](black-swans.html) framing (right for the board; wrong for the earnings call where it sounds defensive).

## What if the board pushes back?

Four meta-questions worth anticipating from a board member who is unconvinced by the framework as a whole:

**&ldquo;Is the analysis itself too elaborate?&rdquo;** No, but it is calibrated for a one-shot 2026 board cycle. The substantive recommendation can be compressed to one paragraph: *endorse the firewall annex with 60-day pre-conditions; authorise capex flexibility for milestone acceleration; defer the patient-access decision pending CMS rulemaking; note the tail-risk hedge.* Everything else &mdash; the bottom-line analysis, the black swans, the financial translation, this Q&amp;A &mdash; is the *defence* of that paragraph, not the paragraph itself.

**&ldquo;Are we overconfident in the firewall annex?&rdquo;** Possibly. The [decision-quality framework](decision-quality.html) shows Branch B (conditional) has higher expected utility than Branch A (full endorse) by CHF 1.3B, a margin within estimation noise. The substantive recommendation defers to Branch A on grounds of strategic posture (long-horizon variance tolerance) but acknowledges Branch B as the conservative ground truth.

**&ldquo;What if we&apos;re wrong about US Executive sitting at BATNA?&rdquo;** The model output shows US Executive surplus = 0 at the May 2026 status quo. If this is wrong (i.e., the US administration actually has strong positive preference for the signed deal beyond what the model captures), the contagion scenario probabilities shift downward (USTR would defend the framework more actively). The framework&apos;s recommendation does not change &mdash; Branch A/B remain dominant &mdash; but the expected utilities compress.

**&ldquo;What&apos;s the simplest thing we could do that would still capture most of the value?&rdquo;** Authorise CHF 5&ndash;8M of working-group resourcing to operationalise the firewall-annex pre-conditions over the next 60 days. Do not formally endorse Item 1 until the pre-condition test results are in hand. This is functionally Branch B; it preserves option value at the lowest decision cost.
