### Roche-relevant signatories highlighted

The structurally most relevant signatories from Roche's competitive perspective:

- **Novartis (#12)** — direct Basel peer; co-marketer of Xolair; PhRMA chair; signed same December 19 cohort. Most aligned counterparty for joint Swiss-pharma front.
- **Genentech (#8)** — Roche's own US subsidiary. **The deal Roche has already signed.**
- **AbbVie (#15)** — Botox is in IRA Round 3 alongside Xolair; setting precedent on combined MFN + IRA coverage.
- **Boehringer Ingelheim (#7)** — only private (non-listed) signatory; family-held; structural counterpart to Roche's family ownership.
- **Regeneron (#17)** — most recent, sets the "current threshold" for what newer deals look like (April 2026 terms).

### Common structural features (per HHS scoping)

- Voluntary firm-level agreements (not federal regulation).
- Scoped to **single-source brand drugs only**; biosimilars and generics are *outside* the MFN regime.
- Pegged to OECD-floor benchmark using countries with GDP per capita ≥ 60% of US (~10 country basket).
- Effective via TrumpRx.gov DTC channel + Medicaid MFN linking.
- Conditional on Section 232 0% tariff exemption track, sunsetting 20 January 2029.
- Each signatory's specific drug list and discount depth is in their 8-K disclosure (see [disclosures register §2](disclosures.html#2-the-mfn-signatory-disclosures)).

### What this table makes visible

Reading the table side-by-side surfaces three patterns:

1. **The December 19, 2025 cohort was a coordinated event.** Nine signatures on one day means manufacturers were negotiating in parallel and signing on a politically-staged date. The framework terms across these nine are likely similar; differences across companies are likely modest.

2. **The non-December outliers (Pfizer Sep, AZ Oct, Lilly/Novo Nov, J&J Jan, AbbVie Jan, Regeneron Apr) are *bilateral negotiations*** — each got specific concessions. AbbVie's January 2026 timing is particularly informative because it followed the December cohort but accepted less favourable terms than December signatories (per analyst reading of the public statements). The lesson: the December cohort was the optimal moment to sign.

3. **Bayer's signature is partial** — pharma-division-only — and is the only one where the public framework is ambiguous about which corporate entity is bound. This is a precedent useful for Roche if it ever needs to disaggregate pharma-from-diagnostics in framework terms.
