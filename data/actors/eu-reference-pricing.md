# EU Reference-Pricing Actors (EMA, G-BA/IQWiG, HAS, NICE, DG SANTE)

> The background actors who don't sit at the US-Roche table, but whose prices and methodologies constitute the MFN benchmark — and who will be hit hardest by any spillover.

## Role

This is a coalition of seat-adjacent actors. None of them negotiate directly with Roche on US pricing, but every one of them appears in the OECD-7/OECD-11 type basket that the Trump administration's Most-Favored-Nation (MFN) executive order of May 12, 2025 uses to anchor US Medicare/Medicaid drug prices ([White House EO, 2025-05-12](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/05/delivering-most-favored-nation-prescription-drug-pricing-to-american-patients/)). The CMS "GENEROUS" model operationalises this by tying Medicaid net prices to the PPP-adjusted second-lowest net price across an eight-country reference basket — UK, France, Germany, Italy, Canada, Japan, Denmark, Switzerland ([Sidley, 2025-12-10](https://goodlifesci.sidley.com/2025/12/10/what-the-us-uk-drug-pricing-agreement-reveals-about-how-mfn-drug-policy-could-impact-the-uk-and-eu/)). Four of the five EU/UK reference-pricing actors profiled here are the *price-setters* in that basket: **G-BA/IQWiG** runs Germany's AMNOG benefit assessment and the GKV-Spitzenverband negotiates the Erstattungsbetrag ([G-BA, ongoing](https://www.g-ba.de/themen/arzneimittel/arzneimittel-richtlinie-anlagen/nutzenbewertung-35a/)); **HAS** issues SMR/ASMR transparency opinions that drive CEPS price negotiations in France ([HAS Transparency Committee](https://www.has-sante.fr/jcms/c_1729421/en/transparency-committee)); **NICE** sets the UK threshold-based reimbursement frontier that is the global HTA reference standard despite Brexit. **EMA** (the regulator) and **DG SANTE** (the policymaker) are different — they don't set prices, but EMA's marketing authorisation gates EU launch and the new Joint Clinical Assessment, which went live January 12, 2025 for oncology and ATMPs under Regulation (EU) 2021/2282, harmonises clinical evidence inputs to the price-setters ([European Commission JCA](https://health.ec.europa.eu/health-technology-assessment/implementation-regulation-health-technology-assessment/joint-clinical-assessments_en); [DIA Global Forum, 2024-08](https://globalforum.diaglobal.org/issue/august-2024/eu-hta-regulation-are-you-ready-for-2025-joint-clinical-assessment/)).

## Constituency

- **EMA**: reports to the Commission, Council, and Parliament; constituency is EU member-state competent authorities and patients/HCPs. Losing constituency = member states bypassing EMA scientific opinions via national derogations.
- **G-BA**: self-governance body under SGB V (§91), composed of GKV-SV, KBV, KZBV, DKG, plus patient representatives (non-voting). Constituency = statutory sickness funds (~90% of German insured). IQWiG is its scientific subcontractor. Losing constituency = Bundestag stripping benefit-assessment authority back to the BMG.
- **HAS**: independent French public authority funded by social-security levy. Constituency = Assurance Maladie, Ministry of Health, Ministry of Economy (jointly oversee CEPS). Losing constituency = a Ministerial Orientation Letter overriding CT/CEESP opinions — the May 5, 2025 letter already pushed CEPS toward "health sovereignty" and renegotiation of the Master Agreement ([Simon-Kucher 2026 LFSS analysis](https://www.simon-kucher.com/en/insights/what-2026-lfss-and-2024-ceps-activity-report-signal-frances-pma)).
- **NICE**: arms-length non-departmental public body of the UK Department of Health and Social Care. Constituency = NHS England, devolved administrations, and patient stakeholders. Losing constituency = Treasury/DHSC overriding TA recommendations via political deal.
- **DG SANTE**: Commission DG under Commissioner for Health and Animal Welfare; constituency = College of Commissioners, Council EPSCO formation, ENVI/SANT in Parliament. Losing constituency = Council vetoing health initiatives on subsidiarity grounds.

## Interests (private)

- **Lower medicine costs for national health systems** — the existential driver of every HTA body; binding budget constraints in DE/FR/UK have tightened 2024–2026. **Weight: 10/10.** ([GKV-SV AMNOG portal](https://www.gkv-spitzenverband.de/krankenversicherung/arzneimittel/verhandlungen_nach_amnog/rabatt_verhandlungen_nach_amnog.jsp))
- **Maintain access to innovative therapies** — political cost of patient access campaigns (oncology, rare disease) is high. **Weight: 8/10.** ([IQWiG on EU-HTA integration](https://www.iqwig.de/en/presse/in-the-focus/european-health-technology-assessment/))
- **Preserve credibility of value-assessment methodology** — being seen as the global "gold standard" is reputational capital; NICE and G-BA actively defend methodology against political interference. **Weight: 9/10.** (inferred from HSF Kramer trilogue analysis, [2026-04](https://www.hsfkramer.com/notes/ip/2026-04/the-eu-pharma-package-finally-here-what-it-changes-and-what-it-means))
- **Avoid being weaponised in US trade negotiations** — strong shared interest; ISPOR and Health Affairs commentary explicitly frame this as a sovereignty concern. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Health Affairs European View, 2026](https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2026.00115))
- **Protect EU pharma sovereignty / industrial base** — Critical Medicines Act and pharma legislation reform make this a Commission priority; HAS in particular has been instructed to factor "health sovereignty" into CEPS pricing. **Weight: 7/10.** ([Consilium CMA press release, 2026-05-12](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/12/critical-medicines-act-council-and-parliament-reach-provisional-deal/))
- **Protect confidentiality of net prices / MEAs** — Germany's Medical Research Act (in force 2025-01-01) enables confidential Erstattungsbetrag specifically to reduce international spillover; this directly clashes with CMS's GENEROUS model demand for ex-US net-price reporting. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Inside EU Life Sciences, 2024-07-12](https://www.insideeulifesciences.com/2024/07/12/germany-amends-drug-pricing-and-reimbursement-laws-with-medical-research-act-drug-pricing-becomes-intertwined-with-local-clinical-research-expectations/); [JS Tindt GENEROUS analysis](https://jstindt.com/generous-changes-everything-cms-mfn-drug-pricing-2025-2026/))

## Positions (public)

- **EMA** welcomed political agreement on the new EU pharmaceutical legislation (Dec 11, 2025 trilogue conclusion); CMO Steffen Thirstrup publicly defended patient engagement in scientific committees ([EMA welcomes political agreement](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/news/ema-welcomes-political-agreement-new-eu-pharmaceutical-legislation); [Pink Sheet/Citeline](https://insights.citeline.com/pink-sheet/legislation/eu-pharma-legislation-reform/ema-sad-to-see-member-states-against-patient-engagement-under-eu-pharma-reform-6FOOXGPUDVGQHKTQNHKNLWSR6U/)).
- **G-BA/GKV-SV**: as of January 1, 2025, the criterion of actual selling prices in other European countries (weighted by sales volume and PPP) **no longer applies** in AMNOG negotiations — a direct attempt to insulate Germany from cross-border spillover ([Value & Dossier German Benefit Assessment](https://value-dossier.com/en/german-benefit-assessment-amnog/)).
- **HAS/CEPS**: 2024 CEPS Activity Report and the May 5, 2025 Ministerial Orientation Letter prioritise innovation access, health sovereignty, transparency, and Master Agreement renegotiation ([Simon-Kucher](https://www.simon-kucher.com/en/insights/what-2026-lfss-and-2024-ceps-activity-report-signal-frances-pma)).
- **NICE**: continues issuing TAs without explicit MFN reference; UK signed a US-UK drug pricing arrangement in late 2025, the most concrete bilateral MFN spillover to date ([Sidley, 2025-12-10](https://goodlifesci.sidley.com/2025/12/10/what-the-us-uk-drug-pricing-agreement-reveals-about-how-mfn-drug-policy-could-impact-the-uk-and-eu/)).
- **DG SANTE**: presented the Critical Medicines Act proposal March 11, 2025; provisional Parliament-Council deal reached May 12, 2026 ([Consilium](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/12/critical-medicines-act-council-and-parliament-reach-provisional-deal/)).
- **EFPIA** (industry counter-voice): DG Nathalie Moll has called the 15% US pharma tariff a breach of a "30-year commitment between governments to protect patients by eliminating tariffs on innovative medicines"; in Feb 2026 called for an EU Office for Life Sciences ([EFPIA on competitiveness](https://www.efpia.eu/news-events/the-efpia-view/statements-press-releases/european-pharmaceutical-industry-calls-for-competitiveness-industrial-strategy-and-dedicated-office-for-life-sciences/); [EFPIA pharma package response](https://www.efpia.eu/news-events/the-efpia-view/statements-press-releases/efpia-response-to-pharmaceutical-package-agreement-more-to-do-to-keep-european-pharma-competitive/)).

## BATNA

National pricing systems continue functioning regardless of any US-Roche deal. G-BA negotiates the Erstattungsbetrag, HAS issues opinions, NICE conducts TAs, EMA grants MAs. **Concrete payoff floor**: status quo plus the additional insulation already legislated — confidential German Erstattungsbeträge (sunset June 30, 2028), Critical Medicines Act provisions for strategic projects and joint procurement, and JCA harmonisation reducing per-country evidence-generation friction. EU/UK actors lose nothing immediately by walking away from any informal coordination with the US; the IRP scrubbing inside Germany already happened ([Pharmaceutical Technology on IRP removal](https://www.pharmaceutical-technology.com/analyst-comment/all-change-germany-confidential-pricing-irp/)).

## Red lines

- **No leakage of confidential net prices into US benchmarks.** If CMS GENEROUS demands ex-US net-price reporting from manufacturers, EU payers lose the rebate mechanism that lets them buy access without publicly inflating list prices. G-BA explicitly designed the 2025 confidentiality option to prevent this; crossing the line means undoing the Medical Research Act compromise.
- **No deal that raises EU list or net prices to protect US benchmarks.** Politically impossible — every EU health minister would be voted out for "subsidising US patients." HAS's CEESP mandate explicitly anchors negotiations in clinically-relevant comparators, not US references.
- **No erosion of EMA scientific independence in marketing authorisation.** A US deal that conditioned EMA decisions on US pricing outcomes would trigger immediate EP/ENVI revolt.
- **No subsidiarity breach by the Commission.** Article 168 TFEU reserves pricing/reimbursement to member states; DG SANTE cannot agree to anything that touches national pricing autonomy without Council consensus.

## Leverage

- **Market size**: ~440M people in EU + UK combined; mandatory market for any global launch.
- **Regulatory gating**: EMA's centralised authorisation is mandatory for oncology, ATMPs, orphans — Roche cannot launch in EU without EMA. JCA outcomes feed all national HTAs simultaneously from 2025 onward.
- **HTA methodology export**: NICE, G-BA, and HAS methods are copied by Canada (CDA-AMC), Australia (PBAC), Brazil (CONITEC), Japan (C2H). The MFN basket countries are the *same* countries whose HTA methods set global norms — leverage compounds.
- **Confidential net-price information asymmetry**: EU payers know the net price; CMS and the US do not. This asymmetry is the keystone of the entire MFN debate.

## Constraints

- **TFEU Article 168**: pricing/reimbursement is national competence; Commission cannot harmonise prices.
- **National statutes**: SGB V (Germany), Code de la sécurité sociale (France), NHS Act 2006 / Health and Care Act 2022 (UK) — each binds its respective HTA body.
- **JCA Regulation 2021/2282**: mandates joint clinical assessment but explicitly preserves national authority on relative effectiveness conclusions and price.
- **EFPIA lobbying capacity**: industry has succeeded in softening the pharma legislation revision (8+1+1+1 data exclusivity rather than the Commission's original 6+2; final agreed Dec 11, 2025) ([Crowell pharma package alert](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/the-new-eu-pharma-package-eu-reaches-landmark-deal-on-pharma-package)).
- **Transparency vs confidentiality balance**: published list prices remain mandatory in DE/FR; confidentiality only attaches to rebates and (now) German Erstattungsbeträge under MFG.

## Timeline pressures

- **2026 autumn**: final Parliament plenary vote on the EU Pharma Package, then Council adoption; enters into force in 2026 ([EMA reform page](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/about-us/what-we-do/reform-eu-pharmaceutical-legislation); [Pharma Focus Europe](https://www.pharmafocuseurope.com/news/eu-council-publishes-final-2026-pharma-package-new-regulatory-framework-for-medicinal-products)).
- **2026 Q3**: Critical Medicines Act pilot programmes launch for oncology and antimicrobials following May 12, 2026 provisional deal.
- **2026**: first JCA reports expected (8 products currently under evaluation as of late 2024 reports).
- **June 30, 2028**: sunset of German confidential-Erstattungsbetrag option under MFG.
- **2028**: JCA expands to orphan drugs. **2030**: JCA expands to all medicines.
- **November 8, 2025** (already passed): MFN voluntary negotiation deadline; rulemaking pathway opens — every quarterly White House MFN fact sheet (Nov 2025, Dec 2025, Apr 2026, May 2026) increases pressure on EU actors to make a defensive move.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions

This is a coalition of distinct institutions with divergent incentives:

- **EMA (regulator) ≠ Commission/DG SANTE (policymaker) ≠ national HTAs (price-setters)**. EMA wants scientific independence; DG SANTE wants industrial-policy levers; HTAs want budget control. EMA's CMO publicly criticising member states ("sad to see" them oppose patient engagement) shows the seam.
- **Germany G-BA**: the single most influential payer in the basket — its IQWiG dossiers shape AMNOG outcomes that ripple to other reference systems. Most "payer-friendly" of the four; least industrial-policy-aware.
- **France HAS / CEPS**: most "industrial-policy-aware" — explicitly factors French manufacturing and sovereignty into pricing per the May 2025 ministerial letter.
- **UK NICE**: post-Brexit, retains global methodology influence but is now an *individual MFN basket country* without EU bargaining cover; the US-UK drug pricing arrangement of late 2025 shows the UK is the weakest link and the most likely to be picked off bilaterally.
- **EFPIA-friendly vs payer-friendly factions in DG SANTE/Commission**: DG SANTE pushed Critical Medicines Act and pharma-package patient-access provisions (payer-friendly); DG GROW and DG TRADE push competitiveness/sovereignty (EFPIA-friendly). The Draghi-report aftermath has shifted the balance toward DG GROW since late 2024.
