# <Actor name>

> One-line tagline describing what role this actor plays in the negotiation.

## Role
One paragraph — who they are in this game. What seat at the table, what authority, what observable behavior makes them a distinct actor (not a sub-bullet of someone else).

## Constituency
Whom they answer to (board, voters, executive, members, regulator). What "losing their constituency" looks like — that defines their political constraints.

## Interests (private)
Bulleted. Each bullet ends with an **importance weight** on a 0–10 scale. Each bullet cites a public source if observable; otherwise marked "inferred."
- Interest 1 — short description of what they actually want here, not what they say. **Weight: X/10.** ([source](url), date)
- Interest 2 — ...

## Positions (public)
What they have said publicly. Bulleted with the statement (paraphrased or quoted) and a dated source link. A position may not match an interest — that's the gap a negotiator works.
- Position 1 — current public statement. ([source](url), date)
- Position 2 — ...

## BATNA
What "no deal" looks like for this actor — *concretely*. Payoff floor. The thing they walk to if the negotiation breaks down. Specific enough to score against.

## Red lines
What they cannot accept under any circumstances, with the constituency reason for each. A red line is not "we'd prefer not to" — it's "we get fired/voted out/sued if we agree to this."
- Red line 1 — what crosses it, what happens to them politically if they cross it.
- Red line 2 — ...

## Leverage
What this actor has that other actors want. Used in asymmetric Nash bargaining as weight.
- Leverage 1 — what they control, against whom it bites.
- Leverage 2 — ...

## Constraints
Legal, political, institutional limits on what they can offer even if they wanted to.
- Constraint 1 — statute/treaty/internal rule that binds them.

## Timeline pressures
Election cycles, fiscal years, statutory deadlines, drug LOEs, court schedules. Anything that makes their discount factor change over the next 2–3 years.
- Pressure 1 — date, what changes.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
If this actor is itself a coalition (e.g., Swiss-side, US executive), name the sub-factions and their points of divergence here. Otherwise omit this section.
