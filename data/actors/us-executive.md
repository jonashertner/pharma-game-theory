# US Executive Branch (coalition)

> Principal pricing-policy actor in the mid-2026 US pharma negotiations: a multi-agency coalition (White House / HHS / CMS / USTR / Commerce / FDA) wielding tariff, statutory and bully-pulpit leverage in parallel.

## Role
The US Executive enters the May 2026 pricing negotiations not as a single negotiator but as a synchronized stack of authorities. The White House sets political direction and brokers the bilateral "MFN deals" with individual manufacturers; HHS/CMS runs the statutory Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Program (IRA) and writes the MFN price-target methodology; USTR negotiates the trade-policy wrapper (the US–Switzerland–Liechtenstein Framework signed November 2025, the parallel EU/Japan/Korea deals); Commerce administers the April 2, 2026 Section 232 pharmaceutical tariff proclamation; FDA controls market access and approval timing. The defining behavior that makes this a distinct actor — rather than a sub-bullet of "the US government" — is that it operates *outside* Congress: every major instrument deployed since May 2025 (the MFN EO, the Section 232 proclamation, TrumpRx, the bilateral company deals) is executive action, not legislation. ([WH EO, May 12, 2025](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/05/delivering-most-favored-nation-prescription-drug-pricing-to-american-patients/); [WH Section 232 proclamation, Apr 2, 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/04/adjusting-imports-of-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients-into-the-united-states/))

Key personnel as of snapshot date: HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. (sworn in Feb 13, 2025, 52–48 confirmation) ([HHS press release](https://www.hhs.gov/press-room/eo-maha.html)); CMS Administrator Mehmet Oz; USTR Ambassador Jamieson Greer ([Senate Finance Wyden letter, Jan 28, 2026](https://www.finance.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/wyden_letter_to_ustr_on_swiss_tariffs.pdf)); FDA Commissioner seat in flux — Marty Makary resigned May 12, 2026, one week before snapshot ([NPR, May 12, 2026](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/12/nx-s1-5819722/fda-commissioner-makary-resigns)).

## Constituency
The President's electoral base (cost-of-living messaging on prescription drugs), the Republican congressional coalition that did *not* repeal the IRA in 2025, cabinet appointees serving at pleasure, the Senate confirmation pipeline, and — institutionally — career CMS/FDA staff with their own statutory duties. "Losing the constituency" looks like: visible MFN deals collapsing before 2026 midterms, FDA approval slowdowns blamed on the administration, or a court enjoining a flagship instrument (MFN regulation, Section 232) before November 2026.

## Interests (private)
- Bank a steady stream of visible "win" announcements on drug prices through Nov 2026 midterms — the December 2025 nine-deal package and the April 2026 Regeneron closeout (17 manufacturers, 86% of branded market) were staged precisely for this purpose. **Weight: 10/10.** ([WH fact sheet, Dec 19, 2025](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/12/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-largest-developments-to-date-in-bringing-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/); [WH Regeneron fact sheet, Apr 23, 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-deal-with-regeneron-to-bring-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/))
- Convert the negotiation into US onshoring: the nine December 2025 deals carried a collective $150B US manufacturing commitment; the Swiss Framework promises ~$200B Swiss investment over 5 years ($67B in 2026). **Weight: 9/10.** ([WH fact sheet, Dec 19, 2025](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/12/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-largest-developments-to-date-in-bringing-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/); [Federal Register, Dec 18, 2025](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/18/2025-23316/implementing-certain-tariff-related-elements-of-the-framework-for-a-united))
- Tariff revenue and the China-substitution narrative on APIs — Section 232 was framed explicitly as a national-security/supply-chain instrument. **Weight: 7/10.** ([WH Section 232 fact sheet, Apr 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-bolsters-national-security-and-strengthens-u-s-supply-chains-by-imposing-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-products/))
- Avoid a pharma-sector stock crash that would feed a "Trump tanked biotech" story; this is why MFN agreements are voluntary deals (not regulation) and why Section 232 carved exemptions for signatories. **Weight: 7/10.** (Inferred from EO structure; see [Crowell tariff alert, Apr 2026](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026))
- Maintain donor-class working relationships with pharma CEOs — the optics are confrontational, the deal flow is collegial. **Weight: 5/10.** (Inferred)
- Keep FDA throughput high enough to claim innovation credit; Makary's Commissioner's National Priority Voucher pilot (1–2 month reviews) was the operational expression. **Weight: 6/10.** ([AJMC on Makary regulatory shifts, 2026](https://www.ajmc.com/view/5-key-regulatory-shifts-from-makary-s-era-at-the-fda))

## Positions (public)
- "Americans will pay no more than the lowest price paid in any other developed nation" — MFN target = lowest OECD-country price where GDP/capita ≥ 60% of US. ([WH EO, May 12, 2025](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/05/delivering-most-favored-nation-prescription-drug-pricing-to-american-patients/))
- 17 manufacturers covering 86% of the branded market have agreed to MFN — first round of bilateral negotiations declared "closed" with Regeneron. ([STAT, Apr 23, 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/04/23/trump-drug-price-deal-regeneron-mfn-negotiations/); [WH fact sheet, Apr 23, 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-deal-with-regeneron-to-bring-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/))
- TrumpRx.gov is the public-facing storefront for MFN-priced direct-to-consumer drugs; launched Feb 5, 2026 with 43 SKUs from AstraZeneca, Eli Lilly, EMD Serono, Novo Nordisk, Pfizer. ([WH TrumpRx fact sheet, Feb 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/02/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-launches-trumprx-gov-to-bring-lower-drug-prices-to-american-patients/); [BioPharma Dive, Feb 2026](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/trump-rx-drug-prices-discount-white-house/811576/))
- Patented pharmaceutical imports face a 100% default Section 232 tariff (effective Jul 31, 2026 / Sep 29, 2026); 15% preferential rate for EU, Japan, Korea, Switzerland/Liechtenstein under signed trade frameworks; 0% for firms with executed MFN + onshoring agreements (effective deferral until ~Jan 2029 for compliant firms). ([WH Section 232 EO, Apr 2, 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/04/adjusting-imports-of-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients-into-the-united-states/); [Crowell alert, Apr 2026](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026))
- IRA negotiation continues: Round 1 (10 drugs) prices in effect Jan 1, 2026 (38–79% discounts off 2023 list); Round 2 (15 drugs, IPAY 2028) prices to be published Nov 30, 2026, effective Jan 1, 2028; Round 3 (15 drugs incl. Xolair, Cosentyx, Botox, Trulicity — first-ever Part B drugs) announced Jan 27, 2026, all 15 manufacturers signed participation by Mar 13, 2026, initial CMS offers due by Jun 1, 2026. ([CMS Round 3 press release, Jan 27, 2026](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/press-releases/cms-announces-selection-drugs-third-cycle-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program-including-first); [CMS Round 1 negotiated prices fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program-negotiated-prices-initial-price-applicability-year-2026); [CMS Round 3 participation fact sheet, Mar 2026](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/cms-announces-manufacturer-participation-third-cycle-medicare-drug-price-negotiation))
- "Foreign freeloading" framing — HHS and CMS publicly position MFN as ending OECD cost-shifting onto American patients. ([HHS press release on MFN targets](https://www.hhs.gov/press-room/cms-mfn-lower-us-drug-prices.html))

## BATNA
If negotiations with a holdout manufacturer or foreign government break down, the executive walks to a stack of unilateral instruments — none of which require Congress:
1. Let the 100% Section 232 default rate hit the holdout's imports on Jul 31 / Sep 29, 2026.
2. Issue a notice of proposed rulemaking imposing MFN by regulation on Medicare/Medicaid (the EO's "fallback" pathway).
3. Pursue drug-importation certification under FDCA §804, with FDA waivers for personal importation from low-cost OECD countries (named explicitly in the May 2025 EO).
4. Public shaming via Truth Social / press conferences naming individual CEOs (the same lever used to force the Sep–Dec 2025 deal cascade after the Jul 2025 "17 letters").
The floor payoff: even if zero further bilateral deals are signed, the executive can claim the 17-of-17 first-round sweep and the Round 1 IRA price cuts as concrete 2026 deliverables. ([WH EO §6, May 2025](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/05/delivering-most-favored-nation-prescription-drug-pricing-to-american-patients/); [Sidley analysis, Apr 2026](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/04/most-favored-nation-drug-pricing-policy-executive-actions-manufacturer-agreements))

## Red lines
- Cannot be visibly described as "backing down" from MFN — the slogan is the policy. Walking back the OECD-floor language would cost the President his signature drug-pricing claim into the midterms.
- Cannot sign a Swiss/EU framework that lets Roche/Novartis/Sanofi pay *less* than US-headquartered firms for equivalent US market access — domestic-manufacturing constituency would revolt.
- Cannot let TrumpRx.gov go dark or be seen as failing — the brand is personal to the President. (Inferred from naming convention; see [CNBC TrumpRx coverage, Feb 5, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/05/trump-rx-white-house-launches-direct-to-consumer-drug-site.html))
- Cannot accept a Senate-blocked CMS or FDA appointment that strips operational control during the Round 3 negotiation window (Jun–Nov 2026).
- Cannot openly contradict the IRA statute while courts have it under review — must continue running the program as written even while attacking it rhetorically (and KFK/Oz have, in fact, continued the negotiation cycle on schedule).

## Leverage
- **Section 232 tariff authority** — bites every foreign-headquartered manufacturer (Roche, Novartis, Sanofi, GSK, AstraZeneca, BI, Novo, BMS-via-EU-mfg). The 100/20/15/0% tiered structure is a discrimination engine, not a uniform tax. ([WH Section 232 EO](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/04/adjusting-imports-of-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients-into-the-united-states/))
- **FDA approval timing and CNPV priority vouchers** — silent leverage; bites every firm with a pending NDA/BLA. ([AJMC on Makary shifts](https://www.ajmc.com/view/5-key-regulatory-shifts-from-makary-s-era-at-the-fda))
- **CMS IRA selection list** — every February cycle, CMS picks new drugs. Selection is statutorily constrained but the *interpretation* of "qualifying single-source drug" is HHS discretion (e.g., the Xolair-biosimilar carve-out decision due by Nov 1, 2026). ([Health Affairs IPAY 2028 analysis](https://www.healthaffairs.org/content/forefront/analyzing-drugs-selected-2028-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-cycle))
- **Bully pulpit** — used in July 2025 ("17 letters") to force the September 2025 AstraZeneca/Pfizer deals and the December 2025 nine-deal cascade. ([PharmExec, July 2025](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/president-trump-letters-17-most-favored-nation-order))
- **Defense Production Act / critical-product designations** — implicit leverage on supply chain.
- **Inter-agency timing** — the executive can synchronize a USTR trade-framework announcement, a CMS pricing notice, and a Section 232 amendment in the same news cycle.

## Constraints
- **IRA statute** — CMS cannot deviate from the Inflation Reduction Act's negotiation framework Congress wrote in 2022. The MFP, the timelines, the eligibility rules are statutory. ([KFF IRA factbase](https://www.kff.org/medicare/key-facts-about-medicare-drug-price-negotiation/))
- **APA notice-and-comment** — MFN-by-regulation requires rulemaking; cannot just be a White House announcement. The EO acknowledges this by *contemplating* future NPRMs.
- **Active litigation** — PhRMA, Merck, Teva, BMS, Novartis, AstraZeneca, and (Feb 11, 2026) AbbVie have suits pending; the Fifth Circuit reinstated PhRMA's case after oral argument in Oct 2025; AstraZeneca cert petition is on the SCOTUS docket. Every CMS move is shadowed by potential injunction. ([Health Affairs litigation tracker](https://www.healthaffairs.org/content/forefront/medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program-litigation-status-check); [Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/appeals-court-resurrects-phrmas-lawsuit-challenging-drug-pricing-provisions-ira))
- **Section 232 procedural requirements** — Commerce must complete an investigation and the President must act on its recommendation; vulnerable to procedural challenge.
- **Senate confirmation drag** — Makary's May 12, 2026 resignation creates a confirmation gap at FDA right as Section 232 implementation begins.
- **Federalism** — Medicaid MFN provisions interact with 50-state Medicaid programs.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
The "US Executive" is six sub-actors with non-aligned incentive vectors:

- **White House (political wing)** — wants visible deal announcements, branded artifacts (TrumpRx.gov, "17 of 17"), and headline tariff numbers. Discount factor: extremely short (weeks). Will trade structural detail for press hits.
- **HHS / CMS (technical wing)** — bound by IRA statute and APA. RFK Jr. is politically aligned with the White House but operationally constrained; Oz at CMS must defend the negotiation program in court even while the President criticizes it. Reported HHS leadership churn (Deputy Secretary O'Neill and General Counsel Stuart exiting Feb 2026) signals friction. ([STAT, Feb 13, 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/02/13/hhs-leadership-changes-rfk-jr-hhs-midterm-elections/))
- **USTR (trade wing)** — Ambassador Greer's instrument is the Swiss/EU/Japan/Korea framework structure. Wants reciprocal pharma market access and protected IP, *not* domestic price control. Senate Finance Democrats (Wyden's Jan 28, 2026 letter) have publicly questioned the Swiss deal's pharma giveaways. ([Wyden letter](https://www.finance.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/wyden_letter_to_ustr_on_swiss_tariffs.pdf))
- **Commerce (Section 232 wing)** — Howard Lutnick's department wants US manufacturing investment commitments; the 100/20/15/0 tariff ladder is its instrument. Operationally distinct from HHS's pricing instrument.
- **FDA** — Makary's tenure showed friction with HHS on approval decisions; the May 12 resignation leaves the agency partly rudderless during the Section 232 ramp and Round 3 negotiation. Career reviewers are reportedly resisting White House/HHS political overrides. ([STAT, May 16, 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/05/16/marty-makary-fda-commissioner-administrative-power/))
- **Career civil service (CMS actuaries, FDA reviewers, USTR career staff)** — runs the actual machinery. Slows political velocity. Most consequential during the Jun–Nov 2026 Round 3 offer-counteroffer cycle.

Congressional Republicans add a seventh, semi-external faction: STAT (Mar 17, 2026) reports MFN itself "divides Trump and Congress" — Senate Republicans worry MFN-by-regulation chills R&D. ([STAT, Mar 17, 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/03/17/mfn-drug-pricing-divide-trump-congress-talks/))
