# US Congress (119th, mid-2026)

> Statutory bottleneck and oversight body: holds the levers (IRA, Medicare, tariffs, tax) that any durable US pharma-pricing settlement must pass through — but is internally split between an executive-deferential GOP majority and an opposition Democratic minority that wrote the existing IRA framework.

Snapshot date: 19 May 2026.

## Role
The 119th Congress (sworn in 3 Jan 2025) is the legislature that wrote the IRA drug-pricing program in 2022 and that retains exclusive authority to codify, modify, or repeal it. Under unified Republican control — Senate 53R-47D (incl. 2 independents caucusing with Democrats), House 220R-215D ([CRS, *Membership of the 119th Congress*, 4 Aug 2025](https://www.congress.gov/crs_external_products/R/PDF/R48535/R48535.3.pdf); [Ballotpedia, 119th Congress](https://ballotpedia.org/119th_United_States_Congress)) — it has largely deferred to the Trump administration's executive-led MFN and Section 232 strategy rather than legislating its own pharma-pricing reset, but has acted in one major area: PBM reform via the Consolidated Appropriations Act 2026 (signed 3 Feb 2026), which delinks PBM compensation from Part D list prices ([Mintz, 6 Feb 2026](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2146/2026-02-06-congress-passes-landmark-pbm-reform-2026-spending-bill); [Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/pbm-reform-within-2026-appropriations-bill-signed-into_law)). The four committees that matter for the Swiss-pharma file are: Senate Finance (Chair Mike Crapo R-ID, RM Ron Wyden D-OR — Medicare/Medicaid/trade), Senate HELP (Chair Bill Cassidy R-LA, RM Bernie Sanders I-VT — FDA, NIH, public-health), House Energy & Commerce (Chair Brett Guthrie R-KY — Medicare Part B, FDA, 340B), and House Ways & Means (Chair Jason Smith R-MO — Medicare Part D, tariffs, tax).

## Constituency
Members answer to (i) voters in their districts/states, (ii) party leadership controlling committee assignments and floor time, (iii) donor networks — pharma/health products is consistently a top-5 industry donor, historically 64% Republican-tilted, with $132M lobbying spend in 2026 to date ([OpenSecrets, Pharma Industry Profile](https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/indus?ind=H04)), and (iv) for committee leaders, sister-committee chairs and the executive branch with whom they must negotiate jurisdiction. "Losing the constituency" looks like Sen. Bill Cassidy's experience: pharma-friendly, vaccine-defending centrist, lost his GOP primary 16 May 2026 to Trump-backed Julia Letlow, finishing third with 25% ([STAT, 17 May 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/05/17/bill-cassidy-loses-louisiana-primary-after-maga-maha-opposition/); [Washington Post, 16 May 2026](https://www.washingtonpost.com/elections/2026/05/16/louisiana-senate-primary-election-results-2026-live-updates/)) — a live demonstration of the cost of crossing MAGA/MAHA on health policy.

## Interests (private)
- Re-election / political survival, with the Nov 2026 midterms six months out and a 220-215 House majority where Republicans can lose only two seats. **Weight: 10/10.** ([Ballotpedia, 2026 House elections](https://ballotpedia.org/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections,_2026))
- Avoiding blame for high drug prices in campaign ads — drug-pricing polls as bipartisan winning issue. **Weight: 9/10.** (inferred from sustained bipartisan PBM and IRA messaging)
- For pharma-state delegations (NJ, MA, NC, IN, PA, IL), protecting manufacturing employment and R&D footprint from MFN/Section 232 fallout. **Weight: 8/10.** (inferred; visible in NC delegation EPIC Act co-sponsorship — Tillis, Budd; [BioSpace, EPIC Act reintroduction](https://www.biospace.com/policy/congress-re-introduces-epic-act-to-remove-iras-pill-penalty))
- Defending committee jurisdiction against executive-branch encroachment (MFN by EO, Section 232 by Commerce, IRA implementation by CMS all bypass Congress). **Weight: 7/10.** ([Dem ranking-member letter, 5 Mar 2026](https://democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/sites/evo-subsites/democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/files/evo-media-document/wh.2026.03.05.letter-re-mfn-agreements.pdf))
- For GOP leadership: maintaining alignment with Trump on signature MFN/tariff agenda while quietly protecting pharma donors. **Weight: 8/10.** (inferred; visible tension)
- For Democratic ranking members: positioning IRA as their legislative achievement and forcing Trump to either codify (giving them a win) or take blame for any rollback. **Weight: 8/10.** ([Pallone-Wyden-Neal-Scott letter, 5 Mar 2026](https://democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/media/press-releases/democratic-health-leaders-president-trump-hand-over-your-secret-deals))
- For Sanders/Warren progressive flank: ideological commitment to Medicare-negotiated pricing and pharma-power containment, regardless of party-line. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Sanders amendment vote](https://www.commondreams.org/news/sanders-drug-prices-amendment))

## Positions (public)
- Senate Finance Chair Crapo and RM Wyden: bipartisan PBM-delinking package is the model — passed in CAA 2026 (signed 3 Feb 2026), reforms take effect 2028-2029. ([Sidley Austin, Feb 2026](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/02/congress-passes-significant-federal-pharmacy-benefit-manager-reform-impacting-pharmaceutical-market); [Mintz, 6 Feb 2026](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2146/2026-02-06-congress-passes-landmark-pbm-reform-2026-spending-bill))
- Crapo at Feb 2026 Senate Finance hearing: PBMs must "not profit above what is their standard service fee, and… have to disclose all their pricing." ([Healthcare Dive](https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/crapo-wyden-to-reintroduce-bipartisan-pbm-package/806012/))
- HELP RM Sanders: held 8 Feb 2026 hearing "Why Does the United States Pay, by Far, the Highest Prices in the World for Prescription Drugs?" with BMS, J&J, Merck CEOs testifying; subsequent floor amendment to mandate Canadian-level Medicare pricing failed, with only Hawley (R-MO) and Sullivan (R-AK) defecting from GOP line. ([Sanders.senate.gov hearing advisory](https://www.sanders.senate.gov/press-releases/media-advisory-help-committee-to-hold-hearing-on-outrageous-cost-of-prescription-drugs-ceos-to-testify/); [Common Dreams](https://www.commondreams.org/news/sanders-drug-prices-amendment))
- House E&C Chair Guthrie + Ways & Means Chair Smith: Smith publicly attacked "borderline extortion" pricing — but by hospital systems, not pharma, on 28 Apr 2026 ([Ways & Means press release, 28 Apr 2026](https://waysandmeans.house.gov/2026/04/28/chairman-smith-to-hospital-system-ceos-the-prices-you-charge-patients-are-borderline-extortion/)).
- Rep. Dan Meuser (R-PA) introduced H.R. 7837 ("Most Favored Patient Act") to codify MFN with statutory enforcement against non-participating manufacturers by Dec 2028 — referred to E&C and Ways & Means; "unlikely to pass Congress this year." ([Sidley Austin, Apr 2026](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/04/most-favored-nation-drug-pricing-policy-executive-actions-manufacturer-agreements); [Meuser press release](https://meuser.house.gov/media/press-releases/meuser-introduces-bill-lower-prescription-drug-prices))
- Bipartisan "EPIC Act" (S. 832, Sens. Tillis, Budd, Blackburn, Daines, Lankford, Curtis; H.R. companion led by Rep. Murphy R-NC) would equalize IRA negotiation timing for small-molecule (9 yr) and biologics (13 yr) — addresses the "pill penalty." ([Congress.gov S.832](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/832/all-info); [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/policy/congress-re-introduces-epic-act-to-remove-iras-pill-penalty))
- Joint letter from four Democratic ranking members (Wyden, Pallone, Neal, Scott), 5 Mar 2026, demanding unredacted MFN-agreement texts from Trump as precondition for any codification. ([Dem E&C letter PDF](https://democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/sites/evo-subsites/democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/files/evo-media-document/wh.2026.03.05.letter-re-mfn-agreements.pdf))
- Trump in 2026 SOTU called on Congress to "codify [his] most favored nation program into law" — no significant Republican legislative response to date. ([Democrats E&C, 5 Mar 2026](https://democrats-energycommerce.house.gov/media/press-releases/democratic-health-leaders-president-trump-hand-over-your-secret-deals))

## BATNA
"No deal" = status quo: (i) IRA Round 1 prices live since Jan 2026, Round 2 negotiated, Round 3 (15 drugs incl. GLP-1s, oncology) selected Jan/Feb 2026 with prices effective 2028 ([CMS HHS Selection](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/press-releases/hhs-announces-15-additional-drugs-selected-medicare-drug-price-negotiations-continued-effort-lower)); (ii) MFN voluntary agreements with 17 manufacturers run via HHS/CMS without statutory backing ([White House MFN savings](https://www.whitehouse.gov/research/2026/05/savings-from-most-favored-nation-drug-pricing-policy/)); (iii) Section 232 tariffs (100% default, 20% with onshoring plans) take effect 31 Jul 2026 ([Crowell & Moring, Apr 2026](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026); [Foley Hoag](https://foleyhoag.com/news-and-insights/publications/alerts-and-updates/2026/april/executive-order-imposing-section-232-tariffs-on-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients/)); (iv) PBM delinking already enacted in CAA 2026. For many GOP members, this is a comfortable BATNA — Trump owns both the wins and the political risk. For Democratic ranking members, the BATNA is also tolerable: IRA stands, MFN exposure attaches to Trump, and they retain oversight leverage. Net: weak Congressional appetite for a comprehensive legislative package before the midterms.

## Red lines
- For pharma-state Republicans (NC, IN, NJ, MA, PA): no statute that imposes MFN with teeth on non-participating manufacturers in a way that closes a major employer's US site — visible in EPIC Act push to soften IRA "pill penalty" rather than tighten ([BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/policy/congress-re-introduces-epic-act-to-remove-iras-pill-penalty)).
- For Cassidy-style centrist Rs (now a politically endangered species post-16 May 2026 primary): cannot openly side with pharma against Trump MFN — costs them their seat ([STAT, 17 May 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/05/17/bill-cassidy-loses-louisiana-primary-after-maga-maha-opposition/)).
- For Sanders/Warren Democrats: cannot accept any IRA modification that narrows the negotiated-drug list, weakens penalties, or substitutes voluntary MFN for statutory negotiation (would surrender 2022 victory) ([Sanders Sept 2025 minority report](https://www.sanders.senate.gov/wp-content/uploads/9.29.25-HELP-Minority-Report-Trump-Drug-Price-Increases.pdf)).
- For Wyden/Crapo Finance leadership: cannot accept PBM legislation that reopens rebate-arbitrage they just closed; the CAA 2026 delinking is their flagship.
- For Trump-aligned majority: cannot pass anything that visibly contradicts Trump's MFN/Section 232 framework, even where pharma donors object.

## Leverage
- Statutory authority over Title XVIII (Medicare), Title XIX (Medicaid), FDA, and tax (IRC §174 R&D, GILTI/§250 affecting Swiss multinationals). Any durable Swiss-US pharma framework needs Congressional ratification to bind future administrations.
- Appropriations power: FY27 cycle (Sept 2026 deadline) is the next must-pass vehicle — historically the carrier for drug-pricing add-ons (PBM delinking rode CAA 2026).
- Senate confirmation power over CMS Administrator, FDA Commissioner, USTR — minority can extract concessions during nominations.
- Oversight hearings as bully pulpit: Sanders' Feb 2026 BMS/J&J/Merck CEO hearing set frame; Ways & Means Smith's Apr 2026 hospital-CEO hearing shows willingness to use the platform.
- Letter-writing / document demands: the 5 Mar 2026 Democratic ranking-member letter on unredacted MFN agreements is information-leverage even without floor votes.

## Constraints
- Senate filibuster: 60 votes needed for non-reconciliation bills — Rs have 53, so any standalone MFN codification or IRA expansion needs at least 7 cross-overs.
- Byrd Rule: limits non-budgetary policy in reconciliation; IRA-style drug provisions barely cleared in 2022 and would face renewed challenges.
- House Rules Committee discipline + 220-215 margin: any 3 R defections kill a bill on the floor (Freedom Caucus has been the active veto bloc).
- Pharma campaign finance: industry contributed ~$89M in 2020 cycle, traditionally 64% Republican-tilted; $132M lobbying spend in 2026 alone ([OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/indus?ind=H04)) — structural constraint on how far either party will go.
- Electoral cycle: every House member and 1/3 of Senate up Nov 2026 — appetite for politically risky pharma votes drops sharply after summer recess.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
Congress is not a unitary actor on pharma. Five sub-factions matter:
1. **Bipartisan PBM-reform axis** (Wyden-Crapo Senate Finance, Grassley-Cantwell on transparency): already won (CAA 2026). Low further appetite.
2. **Pharma-state defenders** (Tillis, Budd R-NC; Booker, Kim D-NJ; Warren D-MA on biotech jobs; Young R-IN; Casey successor in PA): backers of EPIC Act (S.832) and skeptics of Section 232 — bipartisan but understated.
3. **Progressive drug-pricing reformers** (Sanders, Warren, plus House progressives): want statutory MFN with teeth and IRA expansion; ideologically aligned with Trump's MFN rhetoric but opposed to executive-only mechanism.
4. **MAGA/MAHA bloc** (post-Cassidy Louisiana primary signal — Hawley R-MO, Sullivan R-AK voted for Sanders amendment; Letlow, Fleming incoming): pharma-skeptical Republicans willing to break with industry donors.
5. **Pharma-aligned GOP traditionalists** (Smith Ways & Means, leadership): publicly silent on MFN codification, focused on tariff carve-outs and R&D tax — the structural brake on aggressive legislation.

Cassidy's primary loss on 16 May 2026 ([STAT, 17 May 2026](https://www.statnews.com/2026/05/17/bill-cassidy-loses-louisiana-primary-after-maga-maha-opposition/)) tilts the intra-GOP balance away from faction 5 toward faction 4 — relevant for any Swiss/Roche counterparty assuming "the donor money will hold."
