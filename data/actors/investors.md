# Roche investors (institutional, ADR, index, sovereign, sell-side)

> The price-signal actor: no seat at the table, but every Roche move in the US negotiation is graded in real time on the SIX and OTC tape, with the Hoffmann/Oeri voting pool acting as a shock-absorber against short-term pressure.

## Role
Roche's external shareholders — Swiss and European institutional holders of bearer shares (ROG, voting), holders of the newly-listed Participation Certificates (ROP, non-voting, the successor to the Genussscheine as of 17 March 2026), US ADR holders (RHHBY, OTC), passive index funds tracking the SMI and MSCI Europe, sovereign wealth funds (Norges Bank/GPFG, GPIF, ADIA), Swiss pension and life-insurance funds, and the sell-side analysts (UBS, J.P. Morgan, Morgan Stanley, Berenberg, Bank of America, Bernstein) who shape the consensus narrative — collectively constitute the *market-reaction channel* in the mid-2026 US pharma-pricing game. They have no formal authority over the Genentech-MFN deal, the Section 232 tariff response, or 2027 price negotiations, but they price every signal within seconds and transmit a P&L verdict back to CEO Thomas Schinecker and chair Severin Schwan. Importantly, because the Hoffmann/Oeri family pool controls 45.01% of *voting* shares ([Bloomberg / Family Business Magazine, 25 March 2011](https://familybusinessmagazine.com/uncategorized/family-loses-majority-control-of-roche/)), traditional shareholder-activist mechanics that operate at standalone US biopharma simply do not work here. What remains is share-price feedback, AGM votes on compensation/board, and ESG-stewardship engagement.

## Constituency
Active managers answer to their LPs and fund boards on relative pharma-sector performance; passive index funds (Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street) answer to their tracking error and stewardship teams; Swiss pension funds (Publica, AHV-Suva, BVK) and life insurers (Swiss Life, Zurich) answer to plan members and the Swiss FINMA solvency regime — Roche dividends are a material line in their CHF income; GPFG answers to the Norwegian Ministry of Finance and its ethics council; retail ADR holders are price-takers. "Losing the constituency" looks like: a pension fund's CIO being summoned by the board after a Roche dividend cut wipes a noticeable basis-point off the income portfolio; a US active manager underperforming the MSCI Europe Pharma index because they were overweight Roche when an MFN headline hit.

## Interests (private)
- **Total shareholder return on a 1–3 year horizon.** Driven by EPS, multiple expansion, and dividend continuity. **Weight: 10/10.** (inferred — standard fiduciary mandate)
- **Dividend continuity / growth.** Roche has delivered 39 consecutive annual increases, latest CHF 9.80/share approved at the 2026 AGM ([Roche AGM 2026 media release, 10 March 2026](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2026-03-10)). Swiss pension funds depend on it. **Weight: 9/10.**
- **Margin protection** — US is Roche's single largest market and the MFN deal compresses contribution margin per US script. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Genentech US-government agreement, 19 December 2025](https://www.gene.com/media/press-releases/15094/2025-12-19/genentech-announces-agreement-with-us-go))
- **R&D productivity / pipeline value** — giredestrant, fenebrutinib, and the obesity asset are the multiple-expansion story ([Q1 2026 release, 23 April 2026](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2026-04-23)). **Weight: 8/10.**
- **Capital-allocation discipline** — investors want clarity on whether the $50 bn US capex commitment ([U.S. News, 14 May 2025](https://www.usnews.com/news/top-news/articles/2025-05-14/trumps-drug-pricing-order-threatens-roches-50-billion-us-investment-plans-company-says)) is funded out of cash flow or crowds out buybacks. **Weight: 7/10.**
- **Credit rating / balance sheet** — bondholders and equity holders share an interest in keeping the AA/Aa profile intact through the tariff/MFN cycle. **Weight: 6/10.**
- **ESG ratings / pricing-fairness optics** — relevant for GPFG and Article 8/9 funds. **Weight: 4/10.**

## BATNA
Sell. Underweight pharma vs MSCI Europe and rotate to staples, financials, or the European defence complex. The ADR holder's BATNA is *especially* low-friction: OTC RHHBY can be liquidated in a session without the Swiss-franc/CHF settlement constraints of the bearer share. The active fundamental manager's BATNA is to publicly downgrade Roche to a small underweight while staying long Novartis or Lilly. For passive funds the "BATNA" is structurally zero — they must hold to weight — so their lever is stewardship voice, not exit. For GPFG specifically, the BATNA tail-risk is an ethics-council exclusion, which has not been signalled.

## Red lines
- **A material dividend cut.** Would break the 39-year streak and is the cleanest signal that management has lost confidence in mid-cycle US cash flows. Swiss pension funds rebalance and Swiss retail dumps the stock; sell-side downgrades cascade.
- **Credit-rating downgrade** (currently AA-band) triggered by a debt-funded acceleration of the $50bn US capex without offsetting margin protection.
- **Visible loss of Hoffmann/Oeri confidence** — any public signal from the family pool that management has mishandled the US negotiation. The pool controls 45.01% of votes and has held the company together against short-term pressure since 1948 ([Family Business Magazine](https://familybusinessmagazine.com/uncategorized/family-loses-majority-control-of-roche/)); a dissent would be unprecedented and would dwarf any sell-side downgrade in narrative impact.
- **Conceding open-ended MFN without a binding sunset or tariff offset** — any deal that lets HHS unilaterally re-reference Roche's US prices to a moving EU basket without a defined term would trigger long-only underweighting.

## Leverage
- **Real-time price feedback** to management — every Genentech announcement is instantly graded; Schinecker and Schwan watch the tape.
- **AGM vote on remuneration and board.** The 90.18% executive-comp approval in March 2026 is the floor below which management would feel exposed — a drop to the 70s would force visible response under both ISS and Glass Lewis 2026 policies ([Harvard Corp Gov, Jan 2026](https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2026/01/20/iss-and-glass-lewis-2026-policy-updates/)).
- **Sell-side narrative control.** UBS, J.P. Morgan, Morgan Stanley and Berenberg notes set the talking points for buy-side calls with IR; a coordinated cut to Hold/Sell would compress the multiple.
- **Stewardship engagement** (GPFG, large UK/EU pension funds, LGIM-type managers) on pricing-fairness and US-policy disclosure.
- **Bond-market access** — coupons on future USD debt to fund US capex re-price instantly if equity holders signal disapproval.

## Constraints
- **The Hoffmann/Oeri 45.01% voting block** structurally precludes activist break-up, forced buyback, or CEO ouster — investors *cannot* replicate a Pfizer/Starboard-style campaign at Roche.
- **Bearer-share / participation-certificate split**: ADR holders and most retail hold the non-voting ROP (formerly Genussscheine) — they have economic exposure but zero AGM vote. Voting power sits with Swiss-domiciled bearer shareholders.
- **Swiss corporate law** (Minder/VegüV) caps say-on-pay flexibility and requires binding AGM votes on compensation — already structurally binding, not a new lever.
- **SMI 18% cap** ([Wikipedia, SMI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_Market_Index)) limits how much Swiss-index passive flow can amplify any rerating either direction.
- **Index inclusion**: passive vehicles must hold to weight regardless of policy view.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
- **Active fundamental long-only (Capital Group, Fidelity, Baillie Gifford):** have explicit views; will reward credible margin defence and visible US capex execution.
- **Passive index (Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street):** hold to weight; lever is stewardship voting and ESG engagement, not exit.
- **Sovereign wealth (GPFG, GPIF, ADIA, Temasek):** governance-driven, multi-decade horizons; closer in incentive structure to the Hoffmann/Oeri pool than to a hedge fund.
- **Swiss pension funds (Publica, BVK, AHV, Swiss Life general account):** dividend-income-sensitive, structurally overweight given SMI concentration; the most red-line-sensitive sub-cohort on any dividend signal.
- **Hedge funds / event-driven:** smaller float on bearer shares; can play tariff-headline volatility long or short, including paired trades vs Novartis/Lilly.
- **Retail ADR holders (RHHBY):** price-takers; their behaviour shows up as OTC volume on US-policy news days but they exert no governance pressure.
- **ESG-engaged (GPFG, LGIM, Storebrand, large UK schemes):** the only sub-cohort with a coherent public stance on pricing-fairness — supportive of Genentech's Medicaid-MFN framing as long as access is real.
