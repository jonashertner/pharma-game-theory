# Novartis AG

> The second Swiss pharma giant — simultaneous coalition partner and competitive shadow to Roche in the mid-2026 US pricing fight.

## Role
Novartis is the other half of the Basel pharma dyad: a CHF-domiciled, US-listed (NYSE: NVS) innovative-medicines company that shares Roche's host country, regulatory environment, and Swiss diplomatic cover, but is structurally distinct — public-float governance (no Hoffmann/Oeri voting block), a different therapeutic mix (radioligands, immunology small molecules, oncology orals, gene therapy) and, since the October 2023 Sandoz spin-off, no captive generics/biosimilars arm. In the mid-2026 negotiation, Novartis sits at the same table as Roche under the Swiss-US framework, signed its own MFN deal with the Trump administration on Dec 19, 2025, and — through CEO Vas Narasimhan's role as PhRMA board chair — also speaks for the US-domiciled industry. It is at once a coalition partner of Roche (joint Swiss lobbying, shared Xolair franchise) and a rival (every Roche stumble on MFN compliance or IRA negotiation is a relative gain for Novartis's oncology and immunology book).

## Constituency
- **Board of Directors (11 members)**: Chaired by Giovanni Caforio (former Bristol Myers Squibb CEO) since the 2025 AGM, replacing Joerg Reinhardt after his 12-year term. ([BioPharma Dive, 2024](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/novartis-giovanni-caforio-board-chair-appoint-reinhardt/713956/))
- **Institutional shareholders** — fully public float, no family voting block. This is the key constituency difference vs. Roche: Novartis management is directly exposed to quarterly share-price discipline.
- **Employees** (~78k globally, large US footprint in NJ, MA, CA, NC, TX, FL).
- **Basel-Stadt canton** and Swiss federal government — host-country political stakeholders co-mobilized with Roche.
- **PhRMA member companies** — Narasimhan currently chairs the board. ([PhRMA](https://phrma.org/resources/vas-narasimhan-of-novartis-becomes-chair-of-phrma-board))

"Losing the constituency" looks like: a hostile institutional-investor revolt over MFN-driven margin compression, a board chair (Caforio) pulling rank if Narasimhan signs concessions deeper than peers, or a Swiss federal backlash if Novartis's US bilateral side-deals undercut the broader Swiss-US framework.

## Interests (private)
- Lock in favorable MFN treatment under the Dec 19, 2025 deal — three years of tariff relief in exchange for the $23 B investment plan and TrumpRx onboarding of Mayzent, Rydapt, Tabrecta. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Novartis, Dec 19 2025](https://www.novartis.com/news/media-releases/novartis-and-us-government-reach-agreement-lowering-drug-prices-us))
- Protect Pluvicto and Lutathera radioligand monopoly margins — no biosimilar threat, US Q1 2026 Pluvicto growth +76%, $642 M Q1 sales. **Weight: 10/10.** ([Novartis Q1 2026 release, Apr 28 2026](https://www.novartis.com/news/media-releases/novartis-delivered-strong-growth-priority-brands-and-launches-q1-fy-2026-guidance-reaffirmed))
- Defend Cosentyx (FY2025 sales $6.7 B) ahead of IRA Round 3 negotiated price effective Jan 2028. **Weight: 9/10.** (inferred from CMS selection list + Novartis FY2025 6-K)
- Defend Kisqali momentum (FY2025 sales $4.8 B, +58%) as the oncology growth engine while it is also on the IRA Round 3 list. **Weight: 9/10.** ([Fierce Pharma, Jan 2026](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/trump-administration-rolls-out-15-more-high-profile-drugs-ira-price-negotiations))
- Protect the Xolair co-marketing rake with Roche/Genentech — Xolair is on the IRA Round 3 list (negotiated price effective Jan 2028); Novartis books its share of US Xolair royalties. **Weight: 6/10.** (inferred; Xolair on Round 3 list)
- Keep the spun-off Sandoz at arm's length — Sandoz is a separate listed company since Oct 4 2023, but ecosystem entanglements (supply, licensing, biosimilar pipeline overlap) remain. **Weight: 4/10.** ([Novartis, Oct 2023](https://www.novartis.com/news/sandoz-spinoff))
- Maintain Swiss tax/HQ status while shifting 100% of US-bound key product manufacturing onshore by the end of the five-year window. **Weight: 8/10.** ([Novartis, Apr 10 2025](https://www.novartis.com/us-en/news/media-releases/novartis-plans-expand-its-us-based-manufacturing-and-rd-footprint-total-investment-23b-over-next-5-years))

## BATNA
"No deal" for Novartis means walking from a renegotiated MFN ask back to the Dec 19, 2025 contract baseline: three-year tariff relief, TrumpRx onboarding of three specified drugs, future-launch MFN parity commitments. If the administration tries to widen scope (e.g., pull Pluvicto or Kisqali into the direct-to-consumer MFN model), Novartis's fallback is the Section 232 pharma tariff regime — capped at 15% under the Nov 15, 2025 Swiss-US joint statement — plus continuing to litigate or simply absorb Medicaid MFN exposure (the 5–10%-of-sales band Narasimhan has publicly signposted as tolerable). Floor payoff: keep FY2026 guidance intact, preserve Pluvicto/Kisqali list prices outside Medicaid. ([Swissinfo, Nov 2025](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/drug-pricing/timeline-how-us-president-trump-upended-the-pharma-industry-in-2025/90348148))

## Red lines
- **International reference-pricing spillover** — if MFN forces Novartis to publish lower US net prices that European/Japanese/Canadian payers can mechanically reference, the global price waterfall collapses. Crossing this gets Narasimhan fired by the board chaired by Caforio.
- **Compulsory licensing / IP weakening on radioligands or Cosentyx** — would destroy the central thesis Narasimhan has sold to investors since 2018 (focused innovative medicines). Board and major holders would not survive it.
- **Renegotiation of the Dec 2025 MFN deal under duress within the three-year tariff-relief window** — would signal that signed US deals are not durable, undermining the entire $23 B investment justification.
- **Forced unwind of Sandoz arms-length terms** — Novartis cannot accept being treated as a single economic unit with Sandoz for IRA/MFN purposes; the Oct 2023 spin-off would be litigated.

## Leverage
- **Pluvicto / Lutathera radioligand franchise** — no biosimilar pathway, complex isotope supply chain, near-monopoly in mCRPC and GEP-NET. Bites against any administration that wants visible oncology access wins. ([Q1 2026 release](https://www.novartis.com/news/media-releases/novartis-delivered-strong-growth-priority-brands-and-launches-q1-fy-2026-guidance-reaffirmed))
- **$23 B / five-year US capex and 7 new US plants** — including the Carlsbad CA radioligand facility (opened Nov 2025) and the North Carolina flagship (groundbreaking Dec 2025). Politically priced into the Swiss-US framework. ([Novartis Apr 10 2025](https://www.novartis.com/us-en/news/media-releases/novartis-plans-expand-its-us-based-manufacturing-and-rd-footprint-total-investment-23b-over-next-5-years); [Swissinfo Nov 2025](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/drug-pricing/timeline-how-us-president-trump-upended-the-pharma-industry-in-2025/90348148))
- **PhRMA chairmanship** — Narasimhan speaks for the whole branded-pharma lobby in 2026. Bites against the White House and Hill Republicans who need PhRMA cover.
- **Swiss diplomatic muscle** shared with Roche — the combined CHF ~58 B / $73 B Roche+Novartis investment pledge underwrote the 15% Section 232 cap. ([Swissinfo, Nov 2025](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/drug-pricing/timeline-how-us-president-trump-upended-the-pharma-industry-in-2025/90348148))

## Constraints
- Public-company governance with no founder-family voting trust — quarterly disclosure to SEC (20-F, 6-K) and SIX exchange means no MFN concession can be hidden from investors.
- Third Circuit ruling (Sept 2025) closes the constitutional litigation lane against IRA.
- Swiss federal coordination — Novartis cannot break ranks with Roche or the Swiss Federal Council without political cost in Basel-Stadt and Bern.
- IRA statutory ceiling prices apply to Cosentyx, Kisqali, Xolair, Entresto-class selections regardless of any MFN side-deal — these are two separate price regimes.
- Section 232 pharma tariff cap (15%) under the Nov 15 2025 Swiss-Liechtenstein-US joint statement.

- **Jan 2028** — IRA Round 3 negotiated prices effective: Cosentyx, Kisqali, Xolair concurrent hit.
- **End of 2026** — one-third of the $200 B Swiss-pharma US investment must be deployed under the Nov 2025 framework.
- **2028** — three-year MFN tariff-relief window from the Dec 2025 deal expires; renegotiation risk.
- **2028–2029** — San Diego $1.1 B research hub opens; sunk-cost lock-in deepens.
- **Caforio board chair transition** — first full year in seat is 2026; he owns any deal Narasimhan signs.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
- **Narasimhan (CEO) vs Caforio (Chair)** — Caforio is a former US big-pharma CEO; he is unlikely to let Narasimhan over-concede vs Merck/BMS peers but is also more US-political than the outgoing Reinhardt.
- **Swiss HQ vs US operations** — the US country org runs the TrumpRx and Medicaid GENEROUS Model execution; Basel sets pricing strategy. Tension over which side bears margin hits.
- **Pharma division vs former Sandoz** — legally separate since Oct 2023 but supply, transition-services and reputational linkage persist; MFN/IRA optics on biosimilars (Sandoz) leak back onto Novartis.
- **Coopetition with Roche** — joint Swiss-pharma lobbying front (Section 232 cap, framework agreement) coexists with solo IRA negotiation tracks and Xolair co-marketing revenue split. Each company privately benefits if the other concedes more on MFN.
