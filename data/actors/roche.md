# Roche / Genentech

> A signed-up but reluctant MFN counterparty using a $50B US capex pledge to buy three tariff-free years while it manages oncology, ophthalmology and Alzheimer's launches into the IRA era.

## Role
Roche Holding AG is the Basel-based, dual-share Swiss pharma-and-diagnostics group whose US subsidiary **Genentech** is the legal counterparty for federal pricing arrangements. By revenue Roche is a top-3 global pharma (group sales CHF 60.5B in 2025; Pharmaceuticals Division CHF 47.7B, +9% CER) with the broadest oncology franchise of any incumbent and a unique therapeutics-plus-diagnostics structure ([Roche FY25 investor update](https://assets.roche.com/f/176343/x/7bf0314a2a/260129_ir_fy25_en.pdf), 29 Jan 2026). At the table it shows up in two seats: Genentech as one of the **nine manufacturers** that signed MFN deals on 19 Dec 2025 ([White House fact sheet](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/12/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-largest-developments-to-date-in-bringing-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/), 19 Dec 2025), and Roche Holding as the Swiss-domiciled parent whose tariff exposure is governed by the 14 Nov 2025 US–Switzerland framework. CEO **Thomas Schinecker** (Austrian; ex-Diagnostics head, CEO since March 2023) speaks for the group; **Dr. Severin Schwan** chairs the board, re-elected with 97.75% support on 10 March 2026 ([Roche AGM 2026 release](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2026-03-10), 10 Mar 2026).

## Constituency
- **Board of Directors**, chaired by Severin Schwan (Roche CEO 2008–2023, now Chair) ([Roche AGM 2026](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2026-03-10), 10 Mar 2026).
- **the founding family shareholder pool** controlling **a majority of family-held voting rights** through a pooling agreement dating to 1948 — eighteen named individuals and entities including André the founding family (Vice-Chair), a family representative, family branches, and the charitable Foundation Wolf ([Family Business Magazine](https://familybusinessmagazine.com/uncategorized/family-loses-majority-control-of-roche/), 2021; pool composition confirmed on Roche's own disclosures). The pool slipped below 50% in 2021 when a family member exited but remains the dominant voting block.
- **Non-voting bearer shareholders** (Genussscheine, listed on SIX as ROG; ADRs as RHHBY on US OTC) — heavy ownership by global index funds and Swiss pension funds.
- **~103,000 employees** worldwide; the Genentech South San Francisco campus is the cultural anchor of the US business.
- **Swiss federal and cantonal stakeholders** (Basel-Stadt in particular): Swiss federal trade negotiators secured the 14 Nov 2025 framework partly on the back of Roche, Novartis and Nestlé US-investment commitments ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2025/11/14/us-to-slash-tariffs-on-switzerland-to-15-from-crippling-39), 14 Nov 2025).

Losing the constituency means: the family pool publicly siding against management at AGM (vote share below ~90% on a Schwan or Schinecker item would be read as a rupture), Basel cantonal pushback on job moves, or institutional revolt against margin compression.

## Interests (private)
- **Defend ex-US net price** — prevent MFN spillover via external-reference-pricing baskets in Germany (AMNOG), France (CEPS), UK (NICE/VPAG), Japan. Roche's discussions with the US explicitly invoke global cost-sharing: Schinecker said the dialogue was about "a different way and better sharing of cost for innovation… countries with less money should pay less, but also countries which have more money should pay more" ([Fierce Pharma summary of Q4 2025 call](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/roche-reports-softer-expected-third-quarter-while-stressing-loe-endurance-coming-years), Jan 2026). **Weight: 10/10.**
- **Buy down Section 232 tariff exposure to 0% through Jan 2029** — Roche imports the bulk of its US biologics from Basel/Kaiseraugst and Penzberg; the MFN-plus-onshoring track sets the applicable rate to zero until 20 Jan 2029 versus 100% otherwise ([WilmerHale](https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/05132026-onshoring-pharmaceutical-manufacturing-procedures-to-apply-for-onshoring-agreements-to-reduce-section-232-tariffs), 13 May 2026; [White House Section 232 EO](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/04/adjusting-imports-of-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients-into-the-united-states/), 2 Apr 2026). **Weight: 10/10.**
- **Protect Vabysmo, Ocrevus, Phesgo, Hemlibra, Xolair growth curves** — these five were called out as the top 2025 growth drivers in the US ([Roche FY25 investor update](https://assets.roche.com/f/176343/x/7bf0314a2a/260129_ir_fy25_en.pdf), 29 Jan 2026). **Weight: 9/10.**
- **Preserve the Genentech R&D ecosystem in South San Francisco** as the company's primary discovery engine — inferred from repeated Schinecker references to keeping innovation in the US footprint ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/23/roche-stock-earnings-q1-ceo-swiss-franc-us-investment.html), 23 Apr 2026). Marked **inferred**. **Weight: 8/10.**
- **Maximize ROI on the Alzheimer's brain-shuttle franchise (trontinemab)** — Phase 3 TRONTIER 1/2 enrolling at 800 patients each; preclinical Phase 3 added in 2025; readouts targeted for 2028+ ([Pharmaphorum](https://pharmaphorum.com/news/roche-expands-phase-3-plans-alzheimers-drug), Aug 2025). MFN pricing on the eventual launch is existential to that NPV. **Weight: 9/10.**
- **Avoid Xolair value destruction beyond what biosimilars already imply** — Xolair (co-marketed with Novartis) is on the IPAY 2028 IRA list and faces a biosimilar that can market from ~Sept 2026 ([Health Affairs](https://www.healthaffairs.org/content/forefront/analyzing-drugs-selected-2028-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-cycle), Jan 2026). **Weight: 6/10.**
- **Lock in confidentiality of net prices** to avoid international reference contagion — inferred from industry-wide rebate practice and Schinecker's framing of pricing as a multilateral, not a US-disclosure, problem. Marked **inferred**. **Weight: 9/10.**

## Positions (public)
- **MFN signature (Genentech)** — committed to lower Xofluza in the TrumpRx direct-to-consumer channel from $168 to $50, plus state Medicaid MFN access, in exchange for a 3-year suspension of Section 232 pharma tariffs ([White House fact sheet](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/12/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-largest-developments-to-date-in-bringing-most-favored-nation-pricing-to-american-patients/), 19 Dec 2025; [Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/top-drugmakers-reach-us-drug-price-deal-trump-administration), 19 Dec 2025).
- **$50B US capex over five years** — new/expanded manufacturing in Indiana (CGM hub, ~$550M Indianapolis), Pennsylvania (gene therapy), Massachusetts (AI/CVRM R&D), California, plus a fifth site; ~12,000 jobs ([Roche press release](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2025-04-22), 22 Apr 2025; [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/22/roche-to-invest-50-billion-in-the-us-as-tariff-threat-lingers.html), 22 Apr 2025; [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/roche-spend-550m-building-indianapolis-hq-cgm-production-hub), 2025).
- **Constructive but conditional on global rebalancing** — Schinecker on the Q4 2025 call: "We've been in discussions with the U.S. government for most part of this year" and the conversation is about international cost-sharing, not US unilateralism ([Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/roche-reports-softer-expected-third-quarter-while-stressing-loe-endurance-coming-years), Jan 2026).
- **"Roche's U.S. drug prices will be lowered"** — Schinecker's CNBC headline statement on 29 Jan 2026 acknowledged the directional concession publicly ([CNBC video](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2026/01/29/roches-u-s-drug-prices-will-be-lowered-says-ceo.html), 29 Jan 2026).
- **Doubling down on US** — at Q1 2026 results (23 Apr 2026), Schinecker reaffirmed the US investment plan while complaining about Swiss franc strength suppressing reported CHF sales ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/23/roche-stock-earnings-q1-ceo-swiss-franc-us-investment.html), 23 Apr 2026). Group Q1 2026 sales were CHF 14.7B, +6% CER, –5% CHF ([Globe Newswire ad hoc](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/23/3279543/0/en/ad-hoc-announcement-pursuant-to-art-53-lr-roche-reports-strong-sales-growth-of-6-at-constant-exchange-rates-in-the-first-quarter-of-2026-5-in-chf-due-to-the-significant-appreciatio.html), 23 Apr 2026).
- **Participating in IRA Round 3 for Xolair** — Roche/Novartis signed agreements to participate by the 28 Feb 2026 deadline; CMS confirmed all 15 manufacturers in by 13 Mar 2026 ([CMS press release](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/press-releases/cms-announces-selection-drugs-third-cycle-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program-including-first), 27 Jan 2026).

## BATNA
If Roche walked away from the MFN track:
- It would face the **100% Section 232 tariff** on patented imports from Switzerland from 31 Jul 2026 (large companies) — reduced by the US–Swiss framework to a 15% MFN ceiling for Swiss-origin product, but only with country-level cooperation, not the 0% MFN-deal rate ([Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026), 2026; [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2025/11/14/us-to-slash-tariffs-on-switzerland-to-15-from-crippling-39), 14 Nov 2025).
- The realistic walk-away is therefore **15% landed-cost surcharge** on cross-border biologics (Ocrevus, Vabysmo, Hemlibra, Tecentriq, Phesgo). With the US worth ~50% of Pharma sales (US growth +6% in 2025, top region; [Roche FY25 investor update](https://assets.roche.com/f/176343/x/7bf0314a2a/260129_ir_fy25_en.pdf), 29 Jan 2026), 15% on the imported share is a multi-CHF-billion margin hit annually — a meaningful but survivable floor.
- Roche could redirect supply through accelerated US capacity (the $50B plan), but the build-out is 3–5 years; in the interim, ex-US manufacturing is largely unavoidable.
- "No deal" payoff floor: keep current ex-US net prices intact, eat 15% tariff drag, lose the 0% MFN-track preference, and risk being publicly pressured by name (as Eli Lilly and others were before signing). The MFN-signed counterfactual swap gives 0% tariff through 2029 + Xofluza concession + Medicaid MFN access. Roche took it.

## Red lines
- **No legally binding US net-price disclosure that pierces ex-US confidential rebate agreements** — would crash AMNOG, CEPS, NICE and JP NHI reference pricing simultaneously. Crossing this would be a multi-billion-CHF group EBIT hit; the board and family pool would not ratify. **Inferred** from industry-wide rebate confidentiality posture.
- **No MFN scope creep beyond single-source brand drugs into Roche's biosimilar-exposed franchise (Xolair) or into Part B oncology administered drugs (Tecentriq, Perjeta, Phesgo) under terms worse than the IRA process** — IRA Part B inclusion in Round 3 is a precedent Roche will fight to contain via litigation participation if needed ([Health Affairs](https://www.healthaffairs.org/content/forefront/analyzing-drugs-selected-2028-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-cycle), 2026). Crossing here would force Genentech to choose between US market access and FDA approval.
- **No compulsory licensing or march-in on Genentech IP** — Bayh-Dole march-in would be treated as expropriation; the family-shareholder pool would publicly object. **Inferred.**
- **No US deal terms that automatically trigger MFN status in Swiss or EU procurement contracts** — Switzerland's tariff framework is bilateral; importing US MFN concessions into Swiss reference price baskets would unwind the Genentech deal's economics. **Inferred.**

## Leverage
- **Oncology + Dx companion-diagnostic stack** — no other manufacturer can credibly threaten to slow-walk Foundation Medicine companion Dx for new US-launched targeted therapies. Bites against CMS coverage-with-evidence-development pathways.
- **Trontinemab brain-shuttle Alzheimer's pipeline** — only credible competitor pipeline to lecanemab/donanemab with a materially better ARIA-E profile (1% at 3.6 mg/kg, 91% amyloid-clearance at six months; [Pharmaphorum](https://pharmaphorum.com/news/roche-expands-phase-3-plans-alzheimers-drug), 2025). Bites against Medicare/CMS, who need affordable AD therapy.
- **$50B onshoring pledge already credibly underway** — Indianapolis CGM hub, Pennsylvania gene therapy plant, MA AI R&D center; gives Roche standing to demand quid-pro-quo treatment ([Roche release](https://www.roche.com/media/releases/med-cor-2025-04-22), 22 Apr 2025).
- **Swiss diplomatic backing** — the 14 Nov 2025 US–Switzerland framework was partly justified to Bern by Roche/Novartis investments; the Federal Council has political skin in protecting them ([KOF/ETH](https://kof.ethz.ch/en/news-and-events/media/press-releases/2025/11/switzerland-us-memorandum-of-understanding-relief-but-burdens-and-risks-remain-significant.html), Nov 2025).
- **Vabysmo, Hemlibra, Ocrevus US patient volumes** — withholding supply or slow-walking new indications bites against MedPAC and Part B payors.

## Constraints
- **family-shareholder voting pool** can effectively veto strategic shifts; share class structure under Swiss Code of Obligations means the family controls voting without controlling cash flow rights ([Family Business Magazine](https://familybusinessmagazine.com/uncategorized/family-loses-majority-control-of-roche/), 2021).
- **Swiss listing rules (SIX Art. 53 LR)** require ad hoc disclosure of price-sensitive information — any MFN deal that materially changes guidance must be disclosed promptly ([Globe Newswire Roche Art. 53 release](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/23/3279543/0/en/ad-hoc-announcement-pursuant-to-art-53-lr-roche-reports-strong-sales-growth-of-6-at-constant-exchange-rates-in-the-first-quarter-of-2026-5-in-chf-due-to-the-significant-appreciatio.html), 23 Apr 2026).
- **IFRS reporting** plus SEC requirements for ADR holders (Form 20-F equivalent disclosures) limit the ability to keep deal economics off-balance-sheet.
- **EU competition law and German AMNOG mechanics** — any list-price reduction in the US that gets posted (e.g., on TrumpRx) feeds straight into European reference pricing baskets, narrowing what Roche can concede on transparency without cross-jurisdictional damage.
- **IRA Part B negotiation participation is statutorily compelled** absent excise tax of 95% of US sales — Roche has no realistic refusal posture for Xolair ([CMS](https://www.cms.gov/priorities/medicare-prescription-drug-affordability/overview/medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program/selected-drugs-negotiated-prices), 2026).

## Timeline pressures
- **31 Jul 2026** — Section 232 tariffs take effect for "large" pharma manufacturers; Roche's MFN-deal 0% rate applies from this date ([Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026), 2026).
- **Sept 2026** — Xolair biosimilar (Celltrion's Omlyclo / others) expected to begin marketing, eroding LOE economics in advance of any IRA-negotiated price ([Health Affairs](https://www.healthaffairs.org/content/forefront/analyzing-drugs-selected-2028-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-cycle), 2026).
- **1 Jan 2027** — IRA Round 2 negotiated prices take effect for the 15 Part D drugs selected in Jan 2025 (Xolair was *not* in Round 2; it is in Round 3).
- **1 Jan 2028** — IRA Round 3 prices, including Xolair, take effect ([CMS press release](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/press-releases/cms-announces-selection-drugs-third-cycle-medicare-drug-price-negotiation-program-including-first), 27 Jan 2026).
- **20 Jan 2029** — MFN-deal 0% Section 232 tariff exemption sunsets; renegotiation pressure crests just before US presidential transition.
- **2028+** — TRONTIER 1/2 trontinemab readouts; pricing/MFN posture set today will determine the Alzheimer's franchise NPV.
- **Vabysmo / Tecentriq LOE clocks** — Tecentriq biosimilar entry expected in the US around 2028–2029; Vabysmo IP runs longer, but is a top-3 IRA Part B negotiation candidate for future rounds (inferred).

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
Roche-as-actor is a Swiss-parent / US-subsidiary coalition with three identifiable factions:
- **Genentech (South San Francisco) US commercial + government affairs** — owns the MFN deal text and the TrumpRx execution. Most exposed to the US regulatory state.
- **Basel group HQ + Schinecker / Hippe (CFO)** — owns the global pricing posture and the $50B capex narrative. Sees MFN through a global net-price-protection lens, not a US-access lens.
- **the family-shareholder pool + Chair Schwan** — long-horizon owners. Their willingness to absorb US margin compression in exchange for innovation continuity is what makes the deal politically possible. Divergence risk would surface if MFN spills into Europe.
