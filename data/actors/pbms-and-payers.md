# US Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs) and Commercial Insurers

> The middlemen whose rebate-arbitrage business model is being squeezed simultaneously from below (TrumpRx direct-to-consumer), above (MFN/IRA-compressed list prices), and the side (federal delinking + FTC scrutiny).

*Snapshot date: May 19, 2026.*

## Role
PBMs are the formulary-and-rebate intermediaries between drug manufacturers, dispensing pharmacies, and end payers (employers, health plans, Medicare Part D, Medicaid MCOs). Three vertically-integrated entities — **CVS Caremark (CVS Health), Express Scripts (Cigna/Evernorth), and OptumRx (UnitedHealth Group)** — processed roughly **80% of all US prescription claims in 2025** ([Drug Channels, Mar 2026](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/03/the-top-pharmacy-benefit-managers-of.html)). Each of the Big 3 is fused with either a large commercial insurer (Aetna inside CVS, Cigna Healthcare inside Cigna, UnitedHealthcare inside UNH) or, in CVS's case, the largest retail pharmacy chain. In the mid-2026 pricing fights they occupy an unusual seat: nominally on the "payer" side against Pharma in formulary negotiations, but increasingly *adversaries* of the White House MFN/TrumpRx push because direct-to-consumer manufacturer pricing routes around them entirely. Their observable behavior in 2025–2026 — Cigna/Express Scripts announcing a "rebate-free" commercial model ([Healthcare Dive, Oct 2025](https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/evernorth-express-scripts-new-pbm-model-cost-plus/803864/)); CVS pivoting its Medicare bid book around delinking — is best read as preemptive repositioning ahead of statutory change.

## Constituency
Public-company shareholders of CVS Health, Cigna, and UnitedHealth Group (each among the 25 largest US firms by revenue); ~270 million covered lives across the three integrated platforms; employer plan-sponsor clients (the Business Group on Health, ERIC, large self-funded employers); Medicare Part D plan partners; and the PBM trade association **PCMA**. "Losing the constituency" looks like: (a) large self-funded employers shifting to transparent/pass-through PBM startups (Mark Cuban's Cost Plus pharmacy, Capital Rx, Navitus, Rightway) at contract renewal; (b) shareholder revolt if Health Services operating income compresses below mid-single-digits; (c) CMS disqualifying a PBM from Part D bids for non-compliance with the February 2026 CAA delinking provisions.

## Interests (private)
- **Preserve a viable rebate-arbitrage / spread-pricing revenue stream for as long as legally possible**, even as the public model shifts to "pass-through." FTC found Big 3 generated **>$7.3 billion** in excess-of-acquisition revenue on 51 specialty generics 2017–2022 alone. **Weight: 10/10.** ([FTC Second Interim Staff Report, Jan 14 2025](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/01/ftc-releases-second-interim-staff-report-prescription-drug-middlemen))
- **Maintain formulary control** — the single biggest source of bargaining leverage against manufacturers, and the asset that vertical integration monetizes. Loss of formulary discretion = loss of pricing power. **Weight: 10/10.** (inferred from House Oversight findings of formulary steering, [House Oversight, Jul 23 2024](https://oversight.house.gov/release/hearing-wrap-up-oversight-committee-exposes-how-pbms-undermine-patient-health-and-increase-drug-costs/))
- **Avoid disintermediation by TrumpRx.gov** for the highest-margin specialty/branded drugs. OptumRx alone managed **~$87B of specialty spend in 2025**; any TrumpRx expansion of that surface area is direct revenue at risk. **Weight: 9/10.** ([UNH 10-K FY2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000731766/000073176626000062/unh-20251231.htm); [Buchanan Ingersoll on TrumpRx](https://www.bipc.com/trumprx-what-pharmacies-and-plan-sponsors-need-to-know))
- **Defuse FTC antitrust posture** — the Cigna/Express Scripts February 2026 FTC settlement on insulin (no admission of fault, no monetary penalty) is the template they want for the remaining administrative complaints against Caremark and OptumRx. **Weight: 8/10.** ([Healthcare Dive, Q4 2025 recap](https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/cigna-q4-2025-express-scripts-fcommunication-settlement-impact/811438/))
- **Slow the state-level PBM-reform patchwork** (>40 states have enacted some form of PBM regulation 2023–2026) into a preemptive federal floor they can live with. **Weight: 7/10.** ([Mintz, Apr 22 2026](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2026-04-22-pbm-policy-and-legislative-update-spring-2026))
- **Protect medical-loss-ratio (MLR) economics on the insurer side**: lower drug list prices from MFN/IRA actually *help* the insurance arms (less premium pressure), which creates a real intra-firm tension at CVS/Cigna/UNH. **Weight: 6/10.** (inferred; visible in Cigna's willingness to drop rebates first)

## Positions (public)
- "PBMs share the Trump Administration's goal of reducing prescription drug costs… and have repeatedly called on drug companies to lower list prices, which is the most effective and lasting way to deliver a better deal." — PCMA on MFN executive order. ([PCMA via AMCP, 2025](https://www.amcp.org/letters-statements-analysis/federal-update-trump-administration-announces-deal-bring-most-favored-nation-pricing-glp-1s))
- After the **Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026** (signed Feb 3, 2026) delinked Part D PBM compensation from drug prices, PCMA: "Big Pharma Wins Big with Passage of PBM and Employer Mandates" — framing reform as a manufacturer giveaway. ([PCMA press release, Jan 30 2026](https://www.pcmanet.org/press-releases/pcma-big-pharma-wins-big-with-passage-of-pbm-and-employer-mandates/01/30/2026))
- PCMA urged DOL to **rescind** its proposed PBM transparency rule as duplicative of the CAA. ([PCMA, Mar 19 2026](https://www.pcmanet.org/press-releases/pcma-urges-dol-to-rescind-duplicative-proposed-rule/03/19/2026/))
- Cigna/Evernorth publicly committed to **transitioning commercial clients off rebate retention** to point-of-sale savings — a defensive pivot framed as customer-driven. ([Healthcare Dive, Oct 2025](https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/evernorth-express-scripts-new-pbm-model-cost-plus/803864/))
- CVS Health on Q1 2026 earnings call: Caremark is "executing against 2026 rebate guarantee commitments" with "no surprises" expected H2 — i.e., the existing book is intact through 2026. ([CVS Q1 2026 call transcript, May 6 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/06/cvs-health-cvs-earnings-q1-2026.html))
- Big 3 sworn testimony (Joyner/Caremark, Kautzner/Express Scripts, Conway/OptumRx) before House Oversight: claim **>99% pass-through of Medicare rebates** and sub-5% operating margins. ([House Oversight, Jul 23 2024](https://oversight.house.gov/release/hearing-wrap-up-oversight-committee-exposes-how-pbms-undermine-patient-health-and-increase-drug-costs/))

## BATNA
If excluded from the MFN/TrumpRx deal architecture entirely, the floor is: keep the Medicare Part D and commercial-employer book under the new CAA "bona fide service fee" model (effective for 2027 bids, with reporting from July 2028); ride out the specialty/mail-order volume (still ~$87B at OptumRx alone) as a clinical-management services business with thinner but more durable margins; pursue an FTC-Cigna-style no-fault settlement on the remaining insulin complaints. Vertical-integrated players (CVS, Cigna, UNH) can cross-subsidize PBM margin compression from insurance, retail, or provider segments — standalones (Navitus, MedImpact) cannot, so consolidation accelerates. ([Ankura CAA 2026 primer](https://ankura.com/insights/the-consolidated-appropriations-act-2026-a-primer-for-medicare-part-d-plans-and-pharmacy-benefit-managers))

## Red lines
- **Federal extension of Part D delinking to the commercial / ERISA self-funded market** — would collapse the cross-segment rebate-arbitrage model and force write-downs at the Health Services segment level. Boards would replace CEOs.
- **Mandatory rebate pass-through to point-of-sale with full per-drug disclosure** — destroys the negotiating value of confidentiality, which is the whole instrument PBMs sell to manufacturers.
- **Antitrust-driven structural separation** of PBM from insurer or retail pharmacy — i.e., breaking up CVS or UNH. PCMA has consistently fought any such proposal; capital markets would punish severely.
- **Mandatory inclusion of TrumpRx pricing in plan formularies at MFN levels** — would route the most profitable specialty book around the PBM entirely.

## Leverage
- **Formulary control over ~270M covered lives.** A manufacturer that does not pay a competitive rebate can be moved to non-preferred tier or excluded outright — observed in the House Oversight finding of 300 instances of higher-cost-rebate steering. ([House Oversight, Jul 2024](https://oversight.house.gov/release/hearing-wrap-up-oversight-committee-exposes-how-pbms-undermine-patient-health-and-increase-drug-costs/))
- **Vertical integration with the largest insurers** — UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna Healthcare — gives PBMs implicit veto over whether MFN-priced or TrumpRx products get covered as a benefit at all.
- **Specialty mail-order and limited-distribution networks** — the only viable channel for many cold-chain biologics; manufacturers cannot easily bypass without building new logistics.
- **Medicare Part D bid leverage**: PBMs run the bids for the largest plan sponsors; CMS depends on their participation to keep premiums stable into the 2026 midterms.

## Constraints
- **Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026 (signed Feb 3, 2026)** — mandatory Part D delinking, flat bona-fide-service-fee compensation, annual transparency reporting to HHS Secretary beginning July 1, 2028. ([Ankura](https://ankura.com/insights/the-consolidated-appropriations-act-2026-a-primer-for-medicare-part-d-plans-and-pharmacy-benefit-managers))
- **FTC 6(b) investigation** — interim reports July 2024 and Jan 14 2025; insulin administrative complaint Sept 2024; Cigna settled Feb 2026, Caremark/OptumRx litigation ongoing. ([FTC Jan 14 2025](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/01/ftc-releases-second-interim-staff-report-prescription-drug-middlemen))
- **State PBM-reform statutes** in a majority of states post-*Rutledge v. PCMA* (2020) — patchwork licensing, anti-steering, pass-through mandates.
- **ERISA fiduciary litigation** — Johnson & Johnson, Wells Fargo, and others have been sued by plan participants alleging fiduciary breach in PBM contract selection (2024–2026 dockets).
- **MedPAC ongoing congressional mandate** to report on PBM data and recommend further structural changes. ([MedPAC Mar 2026 Ch. 13](https://www.medpac.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Mar26_Ch13_MedPAC_Report_To_Congress_SEC.pdf))

## Timeline pressures
- **2026 midterms (Nov 3, 2026)** — drug-pricing salience is high; both parties want a PBM scalp, limiting PBM political room to push back overtly.
- **CMS GLOBE / GUARD MFN models** (released Dec 2025) — potential 2026 implementation imposes additional mandatory rebates on high-cost single-source drugs, eroding the gross-to-net spread PBMs monetize. ([Goodwin, Dec 2025](https://www.goodwinlaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/12/alerts-lifesciences-cms-releases-mfn-drug-pricing-models))
- **Medicare Part D 2027 bid cycle** — first cycle under CAA 2026 delinking; bids due summer 2026.
- **TrumpRx expansion** — May 2026 White House fact sheet expanded the platform to "everyday medicines" beyond the original 43-drug specialty launch. Each expansion wave erodes PBM-managed volume. ([White House, May 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/05/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-announces-expansion-of-trumprx-gov-to-bring-americans-transparency-and-choice-on-everyday-medicines/))
- **FTC final 6(b) report** — still pending as of May 2026; release would re-energize legislative appetite.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
This is *not* a unified actor — it is at minimum four sub-factions:
- **Vertically-integrated Big 3 (CVS, Cigna, UNH)** — can absorb PBM-margin compression via insurance/retail/provider segments. Cigna has already broken ranks by announcing rebate-free commercial pricing (Oct 2025), reading the regulatory tea leaves earliest.
- **Standalone PBMs (MedImpact, Navitus, Capital Rx, Rightway, Cost Plus)** — *benefit* from delinking and transparency rules that level the playing field; their public positions diverge sharply from PCMA's.
- **Commercial insurers without captive PBMs (most BCBS plans, Humana's residual book, regional payers)** — care about total medical-loss-ratio and premium stability; lower list prices via MFN actively help them, so they are softer opponents of MFN than the integrated PBMs are.
- **Employer plan sponsors as PBM customers** — increasingly demanding pass-through contracts and suing for ERISA breach; this is the customer-side erosion that motivates the Cigna pivot.

The mid-2026 implication: when negotiating with "PBMs," the White House and Pharma both know PCMA's public unity is brittle. Cigna's defection and the standalone PBM faction's tacit support of delinking are the cleavages that make a deal possible.
