# Swiss Federal Council (Bundesrat) — acting through SECO and FDFA

> Switzerland's collegial seven-member executive: the legal counterparty signing any US trade deal, with SECO running the table and FDFA managing the diplomatic frame.

## Role
The Federal Council (Bundesrat) is the Swiss government's seven-member collective executive and the formal counterparty to the US Trade Representative in the bilateral trade negotiations triggered by the November 14, 2025 Swiss–US Joint Statement on Framework for an Agreement on Reciprocal Trade ([SECO](https://www.seco.admin.ch/seco/en/home/Aussenwirtschaftspolitik_Wirtschaftliche_Zusammenarbeit/Wirtschaftsbeziehungen/usa.html), Nov 14, 2025). Operational lead sits with SECO inside the Department of Economic Affairs (WBF), headed by Federal Councillor and 2026 President Guy Parmelin (SVP) ([WBF](https://www.wbf.admin.ch/en/guy-parmelin-president-of-the-swiss-confederation-2026), Jan 1, 2026). The Federal Department of Foreign Affairs (FDFA), under Ignazio Cassis (FDP), provides diplomatic framing, ambassadorial access in Washington, and coordination across the federal administration, but explicitly does not lead the substantive negotiation — Cassis has described his role as "door opener" inside a collective Federal Council strategy ([SRF](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/verhandlungen-mit-den-usa-sind-sie-mitschuldig-am-zolldebakel-herr-cassis), Aug 2025). The 2025 negotiating phase was personally led by then-President Karin Keller-Sutter (FDP, Finance), whose telephone diplomacy with President Trump in late July 2025 became the central political episode of the dispute ([NZZ](https://www.nzz.ch/schweiz/sie-war-sehr-aggressiv-warum-spricht-donald-trump-immer-wieder-ueber-das-telefonat-mit-karin-keller-sutter-ld.1924417), 2025). On January 14, 2026 the Federal Council adopted the formal negotiating mandate for a binding trade agreement ([admin.ch press release](https://www.news.admin.ch/de/newnsb/sUtltWrZR19ixmosRK9RP), Jan 14, 2026; [Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/bundesrat-verabschiedet-mandat-f%C3%BCr-verhandlungen-mit-usa/90774764), Jan 14, 2026).

## Constituency
The Federal Council answers to (i) the Federal Assembly (National Council + Council of States), which elects each councillor and must approve any treaty; (ii) the foreign-policy commissions APK-N/APK-S and the Conference of Cantonal Governments (KdK), both formally consulted on the US mandate ([admin.ch](https://www.news.admin.ch/de/newnsb/sUtltWrZR19ixmosRK9RP), Jan 14, 2026); (iii) the Swiss electorate, which can force an optional treaty referendum if 50,000 signatures are collected; SP co-president Cédric Wermuth has publicly floated launching one ([Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/handelspolitik/wie-sehr-steht-die-direkte-demokratie-den-schweizer-handelsabkommen-im-weg/90995177), 2026); and (iv) peak associations — economiesuisse (cross-sector) and Interpharma (pharma) on the employer side, SGB/USS on the union side. "Losing the constituency" looks like a lost referendum, a Parliament rejection, or a withdrawal of cantonal consent — any of which would force re-negotiation under expired US patience.

## Interests (private)
- Preserve 15% ceiling on US tariffs across all Swiss exports (machinery, watches, chemicals, pharma) — the November framework is fragile and tariff snap-back to 39% is the explicit US fallback. **Weight: 10/10.** ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2025/11/14/us-to-slash-tariffs-on-switzerland-to-15-from-crippling-39), Nov 14, 2025)
- Avoid being framed as captured by Big Pharma; SP and Greens are already attacking the $200B investment pledge as a corporate giveaway. **Weight: 8/10.** (inferred from [Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/handelspolitik/wie-sehr-steht-die-direkte-demokratie-den-schweizer-handelsabkommen-im-weg/90995177), 2026)
- Protect "honest broker" / neutrality positioning to keep Switzerland viable as a diplomatic venue — Cassis has been criticized that neutrality risks becoming "an excuse for political allegiance" ([cash.ch](https://www.cash.ch/news/top-news/ignazio-cassis-setzt-hoffnung-in-weitere-verhandlungen-mit-den-usa-850864), 2026). **Weight: 7/10.**
- Keep the cantonal coalition stable, especially Basel-Stadt/Basel-Landschaft (Roche/Novartis tax base) and Zurich (banking/insurance). **Weight: 8/10.** (inferred)
- Prevent capital flight and CHF over-appreciation that a tariff escalation would trigger; SNB independence constrains direct Federal Council action. **Weight: 7/10.** (inferred)
- Avoid setting a precedent in which a foreign power dictates Swiss domestic drug pricing, which would breach the statutory authority of BAG (FOPH) under KVG/LAMal Art. 52a. **Weight: 9/10.** (inferred)

## Positions (public)
- The 15% cap is a success worth defending: Parmelin announced retroactive effect from Nov 14, 2025, hours after being elected 2026 President ([20 Minuten](https://www.20min.ch/story/bundesrat-bestaetigt-rueckwirkende-us-zollsenkung-auf-15-prozent-103467481), Dec 10, 2025).
- Section 232 pharma tariffs are "an additional burden for the exporting Swiss pharmaceutical industry" — Federal Council expressed regret but accepted that the 15% Swiss cap remains intact ([Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/various/us-imposes-15-tariff-on-swiss-pharma-products/91205417), Apr 2026; [watson.ch](https://www.watson.ch/wirtschaft/schweiz/303750550-bundesrat-kritisiert-us-zusatzzoelle-auf-pharmaexporte), Apr 2026).
- The Federal Council formally adopted the binding-agreement negotiating mandate on Jan 14, 2026, after consulting APK-N, APK-S, and KdK ([admin.ch](https://www.news.admin.ch/de/newnsb/sUtltWrZR19ixmosRK9RP), Jan 14, 2026).
- Keller-Sutter has publicly stated about her July 2025 Trump call: "Within minutes it was clear that he would wipe this deal off the table" — signaling to domestic audiences that Trump deal-making is volatile and any agreement carries political risk ([cash.ch](https://www.cash.ch/news/top-news/karin-keller-sutter-innert-minuten-war-klar-dass-trump-diesen-deal-vom-tisch-wischt-850918), 2025).
- No public Federal Council position links the US deal to LAMal/KVG pricing reform; the Bundesrat has so far treated MFN-spillover into Swiss domestic pricing as a separate health-policy file under EDI/BAG.

## BATNA
"No deal" by the US-imposed March 31, 2026 / July 31, 2026 milestones means reversion to a tariff snap-back trajectory: a return to the 39% reciprocal rate floated in summer 2025 ([NZZ](https://www.nzz.ch/wirtschaft/zollschock-fuer-die-schweiz-antworten-auf-die-wichtigsten-fragen-ld.1896220), 2025), plus full Section 232 exposure at up to 100% on patented pharmaceuticals for any Swiss firm without an MFN-style company-level agreement ([Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026), Apr 2026). Procedurally, the Federal Council's fallback is a WTO Dispute Settlement complaint (slow, currently dysfunctional with US blocking the Appellate Body), coordination with EU/Japan/Korea (each of whom already has a 15% deal), and limited retaliatory tariffs — which the Federal Council has signaled it will not use because Switzerland's market is too small to bite the US.

## Red lines
- No commitment that the Federal Council cannot deliver through Parliament/referendum — a treaty rejected at home would destroy Swiss diplomatic credibility for a generation and end careers ([Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/handelspolitik/wie-sehr-steht-die-direkte-demokratie-den-schweizer-handelsabkommen-im-weg/90995177), 2026).
- No sectoral carve-out for pharma that visibly sacrifices machinery, watches, or chemicals — economiesuisse has explicitly stated it is "not purposeful to focus on individual economic sectors" and intra-employer unity would crack ([Berner Zeitung](https://www.bernerzeitung.ch/zollhammer-aus-den-usa-kann-nur-noch-die-pharmabranche-die-schweiz-retten-447627082634), 2025).
- No undertaking that binds federal authority over domestic drug pricing (KVG Art. 52a, Spezialitätenliste) — that authority sits with BAG and the EDI, not the Federal Council acting alone, and would require legislation.
- No formal abandonment of neutrality (e.g., joining US-led sanctions regimes as part of the deal) — would trigger SVP and pacifist-left opposition simultaneously.

## Leverage
- Roche's $50B and Novartis' multi-billion US capex commitments are formally Swiss-domiciled corporate decisions but were politically credited to Bern in the November framework ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/europe/2025/04/22/swiss-drugmaker-roche-trump-tariff-formula-50-billion-promise-invest-us/), Apr 22, 2025; [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2025/11/14/us-to-slash-tariffs-on-switzerland-to-15-from-crippling-39), Nov 14, 2025). The Federal Council can credibly suggest these commitments are revocable if framework conditions deteriorate.
- Switzerland's role in API/intermediates supply chains: a meaningful share of US-consumed patented drugs depends on Swiss manufacturing (Basel cluster, Visp).
- CHF/SNB and Swiss financial-sector relevance to US dollar clearing and asset management — a side-lever rather than a primary one.
- Diplomatic credibility: Geneva-based good offices and OECD/WTO coordination capacity.

## Constraints
- Federal Constitution Art. 184 (foreign policy is Federal Council competence) but Art. 166 (Parliament approves treaties); important agreements trigger optional referendum under Art. 141.
- Collegiality (Art. 177 BV): decisions are collective, any one councillor can delay by demanding new joint deliberation.
- Federalism: cantons retain healthcare competencies (KV implementation, hospital planning) — Federal Council cannot trade these away.
- Statutory authority over drug pricing rests with BAG/EDI under KVG; binding pricing commitments to the US would require legislative change.
- Parliamentary commissions APK-N/APK-S must be consulted on negotiating mandates ([admin.ch](https://www.news.admin.ch/de/newnsb/sUtltWrZR19ixmosRK9RP), Jan 14, 2026).

## Timeline pressures
- March 31, 2026: US Federal Register deadline for binding text; failure risks tariff re-review ([Swissinfo](https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/bundesrat-verabschiedet-mandat-f%C3%BCr-verhandlungen-mit-usa/90774764), Jan 14, 2026).
- July 31, 2026: Section 232 pharma tiered rates take effect ([Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/trump-administration-imposes-section-232-tariffs-on-patented-pharmaceutical-imports-tiered-rate-structure-takes-effect-beginning-july-31-2026), Apr 2026).
- January 2029: Section 232 exemption deadline for firms building US capacity + accepting MFN-style pricing ([Foley Hoag](https://foleyhoag.com/news-and-insights/publications/alerts-and-updates/2026/april/executive-order-imposing-section-232-tariffs-on-pharmaceuticals-and-pharmaceutical-ingredients/), Apr 2026).
- October 2027: Swiss federal elections — Federal Council composition could shift; any unpopular US deal becomes campaign material.
- Parallel: Paket Schweiz-EU is being finalized for Parliament ([EDA](https://www.eda.admin.ch/de/weiterentwicklung-beziehungen-schweiz-eu), 2026), constraining political bandwidth.

## Notes on intra-actor coalitions
The Federal Council is itself a coalition: 2 FDP (Keller-Sutter, Cassis), 2 SVP (Parmelin, Rösti), 1 Mitte (Pfister), 2 SP (Jans, Baume-Schneider) ([admin.ch](https://www.admin.ch/gov/en/start/federal-council/members-of-the-federal-council.html), 2026). Cleavages on the US file:
- **SECO/WBF (Parmelin, SVP)**: economy-first, prioritizes tariff certainty and Swiss firms' US market access; politically aligned with SVP base that publicly congratulated Parmelin on the deal ([20 Minuten](https://www.20min.ch/story/zoll-deal-gut-gemacht-herr-bundesrat-parmelin-weiss-die-svp-mehr-103450881), 2025).
- **FDFA (Cassis, FDP)**: diplomacy-first, more sensitive to neutrality framing and European reputation cost.
- **EFD (Keller-Sutter, FDP)**: financial-stability lens, scarred by the July 2025 Trump call; reportedly cautious about over-promising.
- **SP councillors (Jans, Baume-Schneider)**: under party pressure from Wermuth's referendum threat; likely red-line on anything that touches social policy or drug prices.
- **Cantonal pressure**: Basel-Stadt/Basel-Landschaft governments lobby through KdK for maximum pharma protection; non-pharma cantons (Bern, Vaud, Geneva, Zurich) push back against sectoral favoritism.
